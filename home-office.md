# Business Use of Home (Home Office)
## Description
Deduction for the portion of your home used for business. Figured on Form 8829 and entered on Schedule C Line 30. About 3.5 million returns claim it (TY 2022).
## Eligibility: two strict tests
The space must be used:
1. REGULARLY and EXCLUSIVELY for business — a room or clearly defined area used only for business. A desk in a corner qualifies; a dining table also used for meals does not
2. As your PRINCIPAL PLACE OF BUSINESS, or a place to meet clients, or a separate structure
## Two calculation methods
1. SIMPLIFIED METHOD — $5 per square foot, up to 300 square feet (maximum $1,500). These amounts are set by the IRS and are not inflation-indexed, so they are the same for 2026. No depreciation, no Form 8829 required, and no depreciation recapture on sale
2. REGULAR METHOD (Form 8829) — deduct the business percentage (office square feet divided by total home square feet) of mortgage interest or rent, real estate taxes, utilities, insurance, repairs, and depreciation
## Notes
- EMPLOYEES CANNOT CLAIM THIS. The home office deduction is not available for unreimbursed employee expenses, which remain suspended. Only self-employed filers use Schedule C Line 30
- The deduction cannot create or increase a business loss under the regular method; the disallowed amount carries forward
- Under the regular method, the portion of mortgage interest and real estate taxes allocated to the office moves from Schedule A to Form 8829 — do not deduct the same amount twice
- Depreciation claimed under the regular method is subject to recapture when the home is sold; the simplified method avoids this
## Related
- Reduces Schedule C net profit; income side is federal/income/business/business.md
- Under the regular method, the office share of mortgage interest and real estate taxes moves from Schedule A to Form 8829 — see federal/deductions/itemized/mortgage-interest.md and federal/deductions/itemized/real-estate-taxes.md
## Required Information
- Square footage of the office space and of the entire home
- Total home expenses: rent or mortgage interest, real estate taxes, utilities, insurance, repairs
- Home cost basis (for depreciation under the regular method)
- Months the space was used for business
## Questions
- Do you use part of your home regularly AND exclusively for business?
- Are you self-employed (not an employee)?
- What are the square footages of the office and the whole home?
- Do you prefer the simplified method ($5/sq ft) or the regular method?
- Is your business profitable (the deduction is limited by business income)?
## Common Errors
- Claiming space that is not used exclusively for business
- Employees trying to claim a home office (not allowed)
- Double-deducting mortgage interest and real estate taxes on both Schedule A and Form 8829
- Creating a business loss with the deduction under the regular method
- Overlooking depreciation recapture when selling the home
## Prompt
- I work from home and want to deduct my home office.
- I have a dedicated room for my business in my house.
