# Family and Dependent Credits
## Description
Credits based on having children or other dependents, or on paying for their care. The largest credit category by volume — Schedule 8812 alone appears on about 47.4 million returns (IRS SOI, TY 2022).
## Credits in this folder
- Child Tax Credit and Credit for Other Dependents (child-tax-credit.md) — Schedule 8812 → Form 1040 Line 19; refundable part on Line 28
- Child and Dependent Care Credit (child-dependent-care-credit.md) — Form 2441 → Schedule 3, Line 2 → Form 1040 Line 20
- Adoption Credit (adoption-credit.md) — Form 8839 → Schedule 3, with the refundable part in Part II
## What changed for 2026
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act expanded this whole category:
- Child Tax Credit made permanent at $2,200 and indexed for inflation beginning 2026
- Child and Dependent Care Credit top rate raised from 35% to 50% beginning 2026
- Adoption Credit made partially refundable (up to $5,000) beginning 2025
- Dependent care FSA limit raised from $5,000 to $7,500 beginning 2026 (an exclusion, not a credit, but it reduces the expenses eligible for the care credit)
- A work-eligible SSN is now required for the taxpayer, spouse, and each qualifying child on the Child Tax Credit
## Questions
- Do you have children under 17, or other dependents?
- Did you pay for childcare or dependent care so you could work or look for work?
- Did you adopt a child?
- Do you and your dependents have work-eligible Social Security numbers?
## Prompt
- I have children and want to know what credits I can claim.
- I paid for daycare last year.
- We adopted a child this year.
