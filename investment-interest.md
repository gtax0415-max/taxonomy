# Investment Interest Expense
## Description
Interest paid on money borrowed to buy taxable investments — most commonly margin interest on a brokerage account. Deducted on Schedule A Line 9, figured on Form 4952.
## Notes
- About 1.37 million returns file Form 4952 (TY 2022)
- Deductible only if you itemize
- Limited to your NET INVESTMENT INCOME for the year (interest, ordinary dividends, short-term gains, royalties). Any excess carries forward indefinitely to future years
- Qualified dividends and long-term capital gains are NOT automatically counted as investment income; you may ELECT to include them, but doing so gives up the preferential tax rate on the amount included
- Interest on debt used to buy TAX-EXEMPT investments (municipal bonds) is NOT deductible at all
- Does not include home mortgage interest (Schedule A Line 8a) or personal interest (never deductible)

- Investment income sources: income/interest/, income/dividends/, income/capital-gains/

## Required Information
- Investment interest paid (margin interest statements)
- Net investment income for the year
- Any disallowed investment interest carried forward from prior years
- Form 4952
## Questions
- Did you pay margin interest or borrow to purchase investments?
- What was your net investment income for the year?
- Did you borrow to buy tax-exempt (municipal) bonds?
- Do you have a carryforward from a prior year?
## Common Errors
- Deducting more than net investment income (the excess must be carried forward)
- Deducting interest on debt used to buy tax-exempt bonds
- Counting qualified dividends and long-term gains as investment income without making the election
- Skipping Form 4952
## Prompt
- I paid margin interest on my brokerage account.
- I borrowed money to invest and paid interest on the loan.
