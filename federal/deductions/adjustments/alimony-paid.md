# Alimony Paid
## Description
Deduction for alimony or separate maintenance paid to a former spouse. Deductible ONLY for divorce or separation agreements executed on or before December 31, 2018.
## The 2018 dividing line (TCJA)
- Agreements executed ON OR BEFORE December 31, 2018: alimony paid IS deductible. Report the amount on Schedule 1 Line 19a, the recipient's SSN on Line 19b, and the date of the original agreement on Line 19c
- Agreements executed ON OR AFTER January 1, 2019: alimony is NOT deductible and is NOT reported
- The rule follows the date the agreement was executed, not when payments are made
- A pre-2019 agreement modified after 2018 keeps the old rules UNLESS the modification expressly states the new (TCJA) rules apply
- These TCJA changes are permanent; OBBBA did not restore the deduction
## Notes
- Failing to provide the recipient's SSN on Line 19b can result in a $50 penalty
- Child support is NEVER deductible and is separate from alimony
- Property settlements and voluntary payments outside the agreement do not qualify
- Available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize

## Required Information
- Date the divorce or separation agreement was executed
- Amount of alimony paid
- Recipient's Social Security number
## Questions
- When was your divorce or separation agreement finalized (before 2019, or 2019 and later)?
- Are the payments alimony/spousal support, or child support?
- Do you have the recipient's Social Security number?
- Was the agreement modified after 2018?
## Common Errors
- Deducting alimony under a post-2018 agreement (not deductible)
- Deducting child support
- Omitting the recipient's SSN (triggers a penalty)
- Deducting property settlement payments

- I pay alimony to my ex-spouse from a divorce finalized in 2015.
- I make spousal support payments and want to deduct them.
