# Penalty on Early Withdrawal of Savings
## Description
Deduction for the penalty a bank charged when you withdrew funds from a certificate of deposit (CD) or other time deposit before maturity.
## Notes
- Reported by the bank in Form 1099-INT Box 2 (or Form 1099-OID Box 3)
- Deduct the FULL penalty even if it exceeds the interest you earned on that account
- This is an above-the-line adjustment, available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize
- Do NOT confuse this with the 10% early distribution penalty on retirement accounts — that is an additional TAX on Schedule 2, not a deduction (see income/retirement/1099-r.md)
## Required Information
- Form 1099-INT Box 2 (early withdrawal penalty)
## Questions
- Did you cash in a CD before its maturity date?
- Does your Form 1099-INT show an amount in Box 2?
## Common Errors
- Missing the deduction because Box 2 is easy to overlook
- Confusing it with the 10% retirement account early withdrawal penalty (which is a tax, not a deduction)
- Netting the penalty against interest income instead of deducting it separately
## Prompt
- I cashed out a CD early and paid a penalty.
- My 1099-INT shows an early withdrawal penalty.
