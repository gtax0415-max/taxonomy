# IRA Deduction
## Description
Deduction for contributions to a traditional IRA. Roth IRA contributions are NOT deductible.
## Notes
- Contribution limit for 2026: $7,500, plus a catch-up if age 50 or older (indexed annually; the 2025 limit was $7,000)
- The limit applies to the COMBINED total across all traditional and Roth IRAs, not per account
- The deduction may be reduced or eliminated if you (or your spouse) are covered by a retirement plan at work AND your MAGI exceeds the applicable threshold. 2026 phase-out ranges: single or head of household $81,000-$91,000; married filing jointly when you are covered $129,000-$149,000; married filing jointly when only your spouse is covered $242,000-$252,000; married filing separately $0-$10,000
- If NEITHER spouse is covered by a workplace plan, the contribution is fully deductible regardless of income
- Contributions can be made up to the tax filing deadline (April 15) for the prior year
- You must have earned income (wages or self-employment income) at least equal to the contribution
- Roth IRA contributions are never deductible here (but may qualify for the Saver's Credit)
- Nondeductible traditional IRA contributions are tracked on Form 8606 to establish basis, so they are not taxed again at distribution — about 3.25 million returns file Form 8606
## Related
- Distributions later come back as income on Form 1040 Line 4a/4b — see income/retirement/1099-r.md
- Self-employed retirement plans (SEP, SIMPLE, solo 401(k)) use Schedule 1 Line 16 instead — see sep-simple-qualified-plans.md
## Required Information
- Amount contributed to a traditional IRA
- Form 5498 from the IRA custodian
- Whether you or your spouse is covered by a workplace retirement plan
- MAGI and filing status
## Questions
- Did you contribute to a traditional IRA (not a Roth)?
- Are you or your spouse covered by a retirement plan at work?
- What is your MAGI (for the deduction phase-out)?
- Are you age 50 or older (catch-up contribution)?
## Common Errors
- Deducting Roth IRA contributions (never deductible)
- Claiming a full deduction when covered by a workplace plan and over the income limit
- Contributing more than earned income or over the annual limit
- Failing to file Form 8606 for nondeductible contributions, causing double taxation later
## Prompt
- I contributed to my traditional IRA this year.
- I put money in an IRA with Vanguard and want to deduct it.
