# Real Estate Taxes
## Description
State and local property taxes paid on real estate you own, deducted on Schedule A Line 5b as one component of the SALT deduction.
## Important: this is part of the SALT cap
Real estate taxes are NOT separately capped. They are added to state and local income (or sales) taxes and personal property taxes, and the combined total is subject to the single SALT cap ($40,000 for 2025, $40,400 for 2026). See salt.md for the cap and phase-down rules.
## Notes
- Deductible only if you itemize
- Must be taxes based on the assessed value of the property and charged uniformly
- If paid through a mortgage escrow account, use the amount the lender actually PAID to the taxing authority during the year, not the amount you deposited into escrow. Form 1098 often reports this
- NOT deductible: charges for specific local benefits or services (trash collection, sidewalks, special assessments that increase property value), transfer taxes, and homeowner association fees
- Taxes on a rental property are deducted on Schedule E as a rental expense instead, and are not subject to the SALT cap
- Foreign real property taxes are no longer deductible
## Required Information
- Property tax bills and payment records
- Form 1098 (may show taxes paid from escrow)
- Amounts for each property owned
## Questions
- Do you own real estate, and how much property tax was paid?
- Were the taxes paid directly or through a mortgage escrow account?
- Is any of the property a rental (deducted on Schedule E instead)?
- What are your other SALT amounts (state income tax) that count toward the same cap?
## Common Errors
- Assuming real estate taxes have their own separate limit
- Deducting the escrow deposit instead of the tax actually paid
- Including trash collection or special assessment fees
- Deducting rental property taxes on Schedule A instead of Schedule E
## Prompt
- I paid property taxes on my home this year.
- My mortgage company pays my property taxes from escrow.
