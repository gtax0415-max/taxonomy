# Medical and Dental Expenses
## Description
Unreimbursed medical and dental expenses for you, your spouse, and dependents, deductible on Schedule A only to the extent they exceed 7.5% of AGI.
## The 7.5% AGI floor
- Only the portion ABOVE 7.5% of AGI is deductible
- Example: AGI $80,000, medical expenses $10,000. The floor is $6,000 ($80,000 x 7.5%), so the deduction is $4,000
- Schedule A Line 1: total medical expenses; Line 2: AGI; Line 3: 7.5% of AGI; Line 4: the deductible excess
## What qualifies
- Doctor, dentist, hospital, and surgery costs
- Prescription medications and insulin
- Health, dental, and long-term care insurance premiums paid with after-tax dollars
- Medicare premiums
- Medical equipment, glasses, contacts, hearing aids
- Mileage to and from medical care, and lodging for medical travel (within limits)
- Nursing home costs when primarily for medical care
## What does NOT qualify
- Expenses that were reimbursed by insurance or paid from an HSA or FSA (that would be a double benefit)
- Cosmetic surgery
- Over-the-counter medicines (except insulin)
- Health club dues and general wellness spending
- Premiums paid pre-tax through an employer plan (already excluded from Form W-2 Box 1)
## Notes
- Deductible only if you itemize, and the 7.5% floor means only large expenses produce a benefit. The 7.5% threshold is permanent and does not change from year to year
- Self-employed health insurance premiums are usually better taken as an above-the-line adjustment instead (see adjustments/self-employed-health-insurance.md)
## Questions
- What were your total unreimbursed medical expenses for the year?
- What is your AGI (to compute the 7.5% floor)?
- Were any expenses reimbursed by insurance, an HSA, or an FSA?
- Did you pay insurance premiums with after-tax money?
## Common Errors
- Deducting the full amount instead of only the excess over 7.5% of AGI
- Including reimbursed expenses or amounts paid from an HSA/FSA
- Including pre-tax employer premiums already excluded from wages
- Including over-the-counter medicines
## Prompt
- I had major surgery and large medical bills this year.
- I paid a lot out of pocket for medical expenses.
