# Itemized Deductions (Schedule A)
## Description
Specific deductible expenses totaled on Schedule A and taken on Form 1040 Line 12 INSTEAD of the standard deduction. Worth doing only when the total exceeds the standard deduction for your filing status.
## Categories in this folder
- Medical and dental expenses (medical-expenses.md) — Schedule A Lines 1-4, only the amount over 7.5% of AGI
- State and local taxes / SALT (salt.md) — Schedule A Lines 5a-5e, capped
- Real estate taxes (real-estate-taxes.md) — Schedule A Line 5b, a COMPONENT of the SALT cap
- Home mortgage interest (mortgage-interest.md) — Schedule A Lines 8a-8e
- Charitable contributions (charitable-contributions.md) — Schedule A Lines 11-14
- Investment interest expense (investment-interest.md) — Schedule A Line 9, Form 4952
- Casualty and theft losses (casualty-theft-losses.md) — Schedule A Line 15, Form 4684, federally declared disasters only
## Important structural note
Real estate taxes are NOT a separate cap — they are one component of the SALT total, along with state and local income (or sales) taxes and personal property taxes. All of these together are subject to the single SALT cap.
## Notes
- You take EITHER the standard deduction OR itemized deductions, whichever is larger
- Married filing separately: if one spouse itemizes, the other MUST itemize too
- Because the standard deduction is high, roughly 90% of taxpayers do not itemize
- Bunching (concentrating two years of deductions into one year) can make itemizing worthwhile in alternating years
- Beginning in 2026, taxpayers in the top 37% bracket receive only a 35% tax benefit from itemized deductions
- Unreimbursed employee expenses and most miscellaneous itemized deductions remain suspended
## Questions
- Do your total itemized deductions exceed the standard deduction for your filing status?
- Do you own a home (mortgage interest and real estate taxes)?
- Did you have large out-of-pocket medical expenses?
- Did you make significant charitable contributions?
- Is your spouse itemizing (if married filing separately)?
## Common Errors
- Itemizing when the standard deduction would be larger
- Treating real estate taxes as separate from the SALT cap
- Forgetting the 7.5% AGI floor on medical expenses
- Missing documentation for charitable contributions
## Prompt
- I want to itemize my deductions this year.
- I bought a home and think I should itemize now.
