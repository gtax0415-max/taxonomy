# Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
## Description
Deduction of up to 20% of qualified business income from pass-through businesses — sole proprietorships, partnerships, S-corporations, and certain rental activities. Also called the Section 199A deduction.
## Notes
- Taken on Form 1040 Line 13, AFTER AGI and AFTER the standard or itemized deduction
- Available whether you take the standard deduction OR itemize — it is never an either/or choice
- Made PERMANENT by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (it had been scheduled to expire after 2025)
- NEW FOR 2026: a minimum deduction of $400 is allowed if you have at least $1,000 of qualified business income from one or more businesses in which you MATERIALLY PARTICIPATE. Both the $400 and $1,000 amounts are indexed for inflation after 2026
- NEW FOR 2026: the phase-in range above the income threshold was widened, which softens the cliff for taxpayers just over the limit
- Generally 20% of qualified business income, but limited by taxable income and, above the income thresholds, by W-2 wages paid and the basis of qualified property. The income thresholds are indexed annually (for 2025 they were $197,300 single / $394,600 married filing jointly); confirm the current-year figures before filing
- Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs) — health, law, accounting, consulting, athletics, financial services, and similar — lose the deduction entirely once income exceeds the upper threshold
- Form 8995 is the simplified form used below the income threshold; Form 8995-A is used above it
- Does NOT reduce self-employment tax — it only reduces income tax
## Sources of QBI (with income-side paths)
- Sole proprietorship net profit — Schedule C Line 31 — see federal/income/business/business.md
- Rental income that rises to a trade or business — Schedule E — see federal/income/rental/rental.md
- Partnership and S-corporation income — Schedule K-1 — see federal/income/pass-through/schedule-k1.md
- REIT dividends — Form 1099-DIV Box 5 (Section 199A dividends) — see federal/income/dividends/qbi/box-5-section-199a.md
- Qualified publicly traded partnership income
Note: QBI is based on NET profit, so the Schedule C expenses in federal/deductions/business-expenses/ reduce the QBI base as well
## Required Information
- Net qualified business income by business
- Whether the business is an SSTB
- Taxable income before the QBI deduction
- W-2 wages paid by the business and unadjusted basis of qualified property (if above the threshold)
- Form 1099-DIV Box 5 amounts (Section 199A dividends)
## Questions
- Do you have income from a sole proprietorship, partnership, S-corporation, or rental?
- Is the business a specified service trade or business (SSTB)?
- What is your taxable income before this deduction?
- Did you receive Section 199A dividends (Form 1099-DIV Box 5)?
## Common Errors
- Believing you must itemize to claim it (you do not)
- Missing QBI from Schedule K-1 or Form 1099-DIV Box 5 Section 199A dividends
- Applying a flat 20% without the taxable income limitation
- Claiming it for an SSTB above the income threshold
- Expecting it to reduce self-employment tax
## Prompt
- I have self-employment income and want to claim the QBI deduction.
- I received a K-1 with Section 199A information.
- I own a small business and heard about a 20% deduction.
