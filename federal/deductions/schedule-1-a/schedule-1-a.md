# Schedule 1-A — Additional Deductions (2025-2028)
## Description
New schedule created by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA) to claim four temporary deductions for tax years 2025 through 2028. IRS guidance released in IR-2026-28 (March 2, 2026).
## The four deductions
- Qualified tips (Part II) — up to $25,000. See qualified-tips.md
- Qualified overtime (Part III) — up to $12,500 ($25,000 joint). See qualified-overtime.md
- Car loan interest (Part IV) — up to $10,000 on a qualifying new U.S.-assembled vehicle loan. See car-loan-interest.md
- Enhanced senior deduction (Part V) — up to $6,000 per eligible individual age 65 or older ($12,000 if both spouses qualify and both have valid SSNs); phases out at MAGI $75,000 ($150,000 joint). Married taxpayers MUST file jointly. See senior-deduction.md
## Key points
- Available whether the taxpayer takes the standard deduction OR itemizes
- These are DEDUCTIONS, not exclusions — the underlying income (tips, overtime) is still reported as income and still subject to FICA
- Temporary: tax years 2025 through 2028 only
- Schedule 1-A must be filed with the return; the total flows to Form 1040
## Notes
- "No tax on tips" and "no tax on overtime" are the popular names, but neither removes the income from the return — both are deductions computed here
- Most have MAGI phase-outs, so high earners may get a reduced or zero deduction
- FILING STATUS: married taxpayers MUST FILE JOINTLY to claim the tips, overtime, and senior deductions — married filing separately is barred entirely
- The tips cap ($25,000) is per return and is NOT doubled for joint filers; the overtime cap ($12,500) IS doubled to $25,000
- The car loan interest cap ($10,000) is also per return and NOT doubled for joint filers
- The senior deduction IS effectively doubled ($6,000 per qualifying person, so $12,000 if both spouses are 65+)
## Questions
- Did you earn tips or overtime as an employee in 2025-2028?
- Did you take out a loan for a new vehicle?
- Were you born before January 2, 1961 (senior deduction)?
- Is your MAGI high enough to trigger a phase-out?
