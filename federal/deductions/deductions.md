# Deductions
## Description
Amounts that reduce income before tax is calculated. Federal deductions fall into distinct layers that apply in a specific order on Form 1040.
## The three layers
1. Adjustments to income (above-the-line) — Schedule 1, Part II → Form 1040 Line 10. These reduce total income to arrive at AGI. Available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize. See adjustments/
2. Standard deduction OR itemized deductions — Form 1040 Line 12. You choose ONE, whichever is larger. See standard/ and itemized/
3. QBI deduction — Form 1040 Line 13. Taken after AGI, available with either the standard deduction or itemizing. See qbi/
Separately, BUSINESS EXPENSES (Schedule C) reduce business income before it ever reaches Form 1040 — they are not part of the standard/itemized choice. See business-expenses/
Plus, for tax years 2025-2028 only: additional deductions on the new Schedule 1-A (tips, overtime, car loan interest, senior deduction). See schedule-1-a/
## Order on Form 1040
- Total income (Line 9)
- minus Adjustments (Line 10) = AGI (Line 11)
- minus Standard or Itemized (Line 12)
- minus QBI (Line 13)
- minus Schedule 1-A additional deductions
- = Taxable income (Line 15)
## Scale (IRS SOI, TY 2022)
- Standard deduction: ~142.5M returns (88.3%) — the vast majority
- Itemized deductions (Schedule A): ~15.5M returns (9.6%)
- Schedule 1 (adjustments): ~65.0M returns
- Schedule C (business expenses): ~35.3M returns
- QBI deduction: ~24.8M returns (Form 8995: ~40.4M)
## Filing status
Several deductions change with filing status — some caps are doubled for joint filers, some are not (creating marriage penalties), and several are barred entirely for married filing separately. See filing-status-differences.md
## Notes
- Adjustments reduce AGI itself, which can also improve eligibility for other tax benefits that phase out based on AGI
- The standard deduction and itemized deductions are mutually exclusive; take the larger one
- QBI and Schedule 1-A deductions do NOT require itemizing
## Questions
- Do your itemized deductions exceed the standard deduction for your filing status?
- Do you have any above-the-line adjustments (HSA, student loan interest, educator expenses, self-employed health insurance)?
- Do you have qualified business income (QBI)?
- Did you earn qualified tips or overtime (2025-2028 Schedule 1-A deductions)?
## Prompt
- I want to know whether to itemize or take the standard deduction.
- What deductions can I claim this year?
