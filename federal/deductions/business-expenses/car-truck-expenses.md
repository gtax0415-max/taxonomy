# Car and Truck Expenses
## Description
Cost of operating a vehicle for business. Deducted on Schedule C Line 9. The most commonly claimed Schedule C expense — about 13.7 million returns (roughly 39% of Schedule C filers, TY 2022).
## Two methods (choose one)
1. STANDARD MILEAGE RATE — multiply business miles by the IRS rate. For 2026 the business rate is 72.5 cents per mile from January 1 (up 2.5 cents from the 70 cents used in 2025); a mid-year increase to 76 cents per mile was announced effective July 1, 2026, so miles in the second half of 2026 use the higher rate — track first-half and second-half miles separately. Simpler than actual expenses and requires only a mileage log. Parking and tolls can be added on top
2. ACTUAL EXPENSES — deduct the business percentage of gas, oil, repairs, insurance, registration, lease payments, and depreciation
## Notes
- Commuting between home and a regular workplace is NEVER deductible; travel between business locations is
- If you want the option to use the standard mileage rate later, you must use it in the FIRST year the vehicle is placed in service
- Once Section 179 or bonus depreciation is claimed on a vehicle, you cannot switch to the standard mileage rate for that vehicle in later years — you must continue with actual expenses
- A written mileage log (date, destination, business purpose, miles) is required substantiation; reconstructed logs are frequently disallowed on audit
- Passenger autos are subject to annual depreciation dollar caps under Section 280F; heavy SUVs over 6,000 lbs GVWR have different rules
## Related
- Reduces Schedule C net profit; income side is federal/income/business/ (see business.md for the overview)
- Employees generally CANNOT deduct unreimbursed mileage; the disallowance was made permanent by OBBBA. Request an accountable-plan reimbursement from the employer instead
## Required Information
- Total miles driven and business miles driven for the year
- Mileage log with dates, destinations, and business purposes
- If using actual expenses: receipts for gas, repairs, insurance, and the vehicle's cost basis
- Date the vehicle was placed in service
## Questions
- How many business miles did you drive, and do you have a log?
- Do you want to use the standard mileage rate or actual expenses?
- What percentage of the vehicle's use is business?
- Have you previously claimed depreciation or Section 179 on this vehicle?
## Common Errors
- Deducting commuting miles
- Having no contemporaneous mileage log
- Switching to the standard mileage rate after claiming Section 179 or bonus depreciation
- Deducting 100% of vehicle costs when there is personal use
- Claiming both the standard mileage rate and actual expenses for the same vehicle
## Prompt
- I drive my car for work and want to deduct the mileage.
- I use my truck for my business.
- I deliver for a rideshare or delivery app and drive a lot.
