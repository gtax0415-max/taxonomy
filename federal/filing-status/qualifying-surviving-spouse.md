# Qualifying Surviving Spouse (QSS)
## Description
A status that lets a recent widow or widower keep the married filing jointly standard deduction and brackets for two years after the year of the spouse's death. Formerly called "qualifying widow(er)."
## The timeline — three distinct years
- YEAR OF DEATH: you generally file MARRIED FILING JOINTLY, not QSS. The final joint return with the deceased spouse
- THE NEXT TWO YEARS: qualifying surviving spouse, if you meet the tests below
- YEAR THREE AND AFTER: usually HEAD OF HOUSEHOLD if you still have a qualifying person, otherwise single
## Requirements
- Your spouse died in one of the TWO preceding tax years
- You have not REMARRIED as of the end of the tax year
- You have a DEPENDENT CHILD — a son, daughter, stepchild, or adopted child — who lived with you all year, excluding temporary absences. A foster child does NOT count, and a dependent parent or other relative does NOT count
- You paid MORE THAN HALF the cost of keeping up that home
- You were eligible to file a joint return in the year of death, even if you did not
## 2026 amounts
- Standard deduction: $32,200, the same as married filing jointly
- Additional standard deduction for age 65 or older, or blindness: $1,650 per condition
## Why this beats head of household
Both statuses may be available, but QSS is generally better while it lasts:
- Standard deduction $32,200 versus $24,150 for 2026 — a difference of $8,050
- Joint tax brackets, which are wider at every rate. The gap grows with income
Head of household has no expiration and accepts a wider range of qualifying persons, so most widows and widowers move to it in year three.
## Notes
- The DEPENDENT CHILD requirement is stricter than head of household. A widow supporting a dependent parent or a foster child qualifies for head of household but NOT for qualifying surviving spouse
- Remarrying ends QSS immediately. You then file with your new spouse
- The child must live with you ALL YEAR, not just more than half
## Questions
- In which year did your spouse die?
- Have you remarried?
- Do you have a son, daughter, stepchild, or adopted child who lived with you all year and is your dependent?
- Did you pay more than half the cost of keeping up the home?
## Common Errors
- Using QSS in the year of death instead of filing a final joint return
- Claiming it in year three or later, after the two-year window closes
- Claiming it with a foster child, a dependent parent, or another relative who does not meet the stricter child requirement
- Defaulting to single or head of household during the two QSS years and losing $8,050 of standard deduction
- Continuing to claim it after remarrying
## Prompt
- My spouse died two years ago and I have a child at home.
- I am widowed with dependent children.
- What filing status do I use after my husband passed away?
