# Form 6781 — Section 1256 Contracts and Straddles
## Description
Reports gains and losses from Section 1256 contracts — regulated futures contracts, foreign currency contracts, nonequity options, dealer equity options — and from straddles. About 624,000 returns file it (IRS SOI, TY 2022).
## Two rules that make Section 1256 unusual
1. MARK TO MARKET: open contracts are treated as SOLD at fair market value on the last business day of the year, so unrealized gains and losses are recognized annually even if you never closed the position
2. THE 60/40 RULE: regardless of how long you actually held the contract, 60% of the gain or loss is treated as LONG-TERM and 40% as SHORT-TERM. A contract held for one day still gets 60% long-term treatment
The 60/40 split produces a blended rate that is usually more favorable than pure short-term treatment.
## Net Section 1256 loss carryback election
Unlike most capital losses, a net Section 1256 loss may be CARRIED BACK three years — but only against Section 1256 gains in those years. This is an election, made on Form 6781.
## Straddles
Offsetting positions in personal property are subject to loss deferral rules: a loss on one leg of a straddle is deferred to the extent of unrecognized gain in the offsetting position. Parts II and III of the form handle these.
## Notes
- Section 1256 treatment applies whether or not the contract was closed, so brokers report an aggregate profit or loss figure on Form 1099-B
- Equity options on individual stocks are generally NOT Section 1256 contracts; broad-based index options generally are
- Hedging transactions in a trade or business are excluded from Section 1256 treatment and follow ordinary rules
## Required Information
- Form 1099-B aggregate profit or loss on Section 1256 contracts
- Positions held open at year end and their fair market values
- Any straddle positions and unrecognized gains
- Prior-year Section 1256 gains if making the carryback election
## Questions
- Did you trade futures, broad-based index options, or foreign currency contracts?
- Do you have open Section 1256 positions at year end?
- Did you hold offsetting positions (straddles)?
- Do you have a net Section 1256 loss you want to carry back three years?
## Common Errors
- Reporting Section 1256 contracts on Form 8949 as ordinary capital transactions
- Ignoring the year-end mark to market on open positions
- Applying actual holding period instead of the automatic 60/40 split
- Missing the three-year carryback election for a net Section 1256 loss
## Prompt
- I trade futures contracts.
- I have Section 1256 contracts on my 1099-B.
- I trade index options.
