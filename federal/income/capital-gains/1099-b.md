# Form 1099-B
## Description
Proceeds from broker and barter exchange transactions, used to report capital gains and losses from selling stocks, bonds, or other securities.
## Required Information
- Payer (broker) name
- Payer TIN
- Description of property sold
- Date acquired and date sold
- Proceeds
- Cost basis
- Whether basis was reported to the IRS (covered vs noncovered)
## Notes
- Exception 1 (Form 8949): if the 1099-B shows basis was reported to the IRS with no adjustments in Box 1f or 1g, you may report the totals directly on Schedule D, Line 1a or 8a and skip Form 8949 entirely
- Schedule D is still filed even when Form 8949 is skipped
- Exception 1 cannot be used for collectibles, wash sales, or other transactions that require an adjustment
## Questions
- Did you sell any stocks, bonds, or other securities this year?
- Was the cost basis reported to the IRS on your 1099-B (covered)?
- Were there any adjustments (wash sales, collectibles, etc.)?
- Were the sales short-term (held one year or less) or long-term?
## Common Errors
- Missing cost basis
- Confusing short-term and long-term transactions
- Forgetting to report a sale
- Using Exception 1 when an adjustment (e.g., wash sale) actually applies
## Prompt
- I sold some stocks this year and received a 1099-B from my broker.
- My brokerage sent me a 1099-B showing basis reported to the IRS.
- I have covered transactions with no adjustments on my 1099-B.
- I sold shares of a mutual fund and got a 1099-B.
