# Form 1099-R
## Description
Distributions from pensions, annuities, retirement or profit-sharing plans, IRAs, and insurance contracts.
## Which line does it go on?
The Form 1099-R Box 7b "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" checkbox decides whether the distribution is reported on Form 1040 Line 4 (IRAs) or Form 1040 Line 5 (pensions and annuities):
- Form 1099-R Box 7b CHECKED → IRA-type distribution → use Form 1040 Line 4
- Form 1099-R Box 7b NOT checked → pension, annuity, or employer plan (401(k), 403(b), etc.) → use Form 1040 Line 5
## Box-to-line mapping
- Form 1099-R Box 1 (gross distribution) → Form 1040 Line 4a (if IRA) or Form 1040 Line 5a (if pension/annuity)
- Form 1099-R Box 2a (taxable amount) → Form 1040 Line 4b (if IRA) or Form 1040 Line 5b (if pension/annuity)
- Form 1099-R Box 4 (federal income tax withheld) → Form 1040 Line 25b (this is tax already paid; it is credited on your return)
- Form 1099-R Box 7a (distribution code) → identifies the type of distribution and affects taxability (see codes below)
- Form 1099-R Box 7b (IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox) → checked = Form 1040 Line 4; unchecked = Form 1040 Line 5
## Box 7a distribution codes (common)
- 1 = Early distribution, no known exception (under age 59.5) → the taxable amount is generally subject to the 10% additional tax (Form 5329 / Schedule 2)
- 2 = Early distribution, exception applies → early, but the 10% additional tax does not apply
- 3 = Disability → may be reported as wages on Form 1040 if under the plan's minimum retirement age
- 4 = Death → distribution to a beneficiary (inherited); not subject to the 10% early-distribution tax
- 7 = Normal distribution (age 59.5 or older) → no early-distribution penalty
- G = Direct rollover to another retirement plan or IRA → generally not taxable (Form 1040 Line 4b / 5b is usually 0)
- B = Designated Roth account distribution
- H = Direct rollover of a designated Roth account to a Roth IRA → generally not taxable
- (The full list is printed on the back of Form 1099-R)
## Notes
- The "a" line (Form 1040 Line 4a / 5a) is the gross amount from Form 1099-R Box 1; the "b" line (Form 1040 Line 4b / 5b) is the taxable amount from Form 1099-R Box 2a
- Traditional IRA and 401(k) withdrawals are usually 100% taxable; if after-tax contributions were made, part is tax-free (Simplified Method or General Rule), so Form 1099-R Box 2a is less than Form 1099-R Box 1
- If Form 1099-R Box 2a is blank and "taxable amount not determined" (Form 1099-R Box 2b) is checked, the taxable amount must be figured separately
- If you ever made NONDEDUCTIBLE traditional IRA contributions, part of each distribution is tax-free. That basis is tracked on Form 8606 — see nondeductible-ira-basis.md. Without it, the same money is taxed twice
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin at age 73 (age 75 for those born in 1960 or later) under SECURE 2.0; missing an RMD triggers an excise tax
## Required Information
- Payer name and TIN
- Gross distribution (Form 1099-R Box 1)
- Taxable amount (Form 1099-R Box 2a) and whether "not determined" (Form 1099-R Box 2b) is checked
- Distribution code (Form 1099-R Box 7a)
- Whether the Form 1099-R Box 7b IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox is checked
- Federal income tax withheld (Form 1099-R Box 4)
## Questions
- Is the Form 1099-R Box 7b IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox checked (IRA → Form 1040 Line 4) or not (pension/annuity → Form 1040 Line 5)?
- What is the Form 1099-R Box 7a distribution code?
- Did you make any after-tax contributions to the plan (so part may be tax-free)?
- Were you under age 59.5 when you took the distribution?
## Common Errors
- Reporting the gross amount (Form 1099-R Box 1) as taxable when part is tax-free (after-tax basis)
- Putting an IRA distribution on Form 1040 Line 5 (or a pension on Form 1040 Line 4) by ignoring the Form 1099-R Box 7b checkbox
- Forgetting to claim Form 1099-R Box 4 withholding on Form 1040 Line 25b
- Missing the 10% early-distribution penalty on a code 1 distribution
- Missing a Required Minimum Distribution
## Prompt
- I took money out of my 401(k) this year and got a 1099-R.
- I receive a pension and got a 1099-R.
- I took an IRA distribution and received a 1099-R.
- I rolled over my retirement account and got a 1099-R.
