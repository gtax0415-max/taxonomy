# Form 1099-R
## Description
Distributions from pensions, annuities, retirement or profit-sharing plans, IRAs, and insurance contracts.
## Which line does it go on?
The Form 1099-R "IRA/SEP/SIMPLE" checkbox (next to Form 1099-R Box 7) decides whether the distribution is reported on Form 1040 Line 4 (IRAs) or Form 1040 Line 5 (pensions and annuities):
- Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box CHECKED → IRA-type distribution → use Form 1040 Line 4
- Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box NOT checked → pension, annuity, or employer plan (401(k), 403(b), etc.) → use Form 1040 Line 5
## Box-to-line mapping
- Form 1099-R Box 1 (gross distribution) → Form 1040 Line 4a (if IRA) or Form 1040 Line 5a (if pension/annuity)
- Form 1099-R Box 2a (taxable amount) → Form 1040 Line 4b (if IRA) or Form 1040 Line 5b (if pension/annuity)
- Form 1099-R Box 4 (federal income tax withheld) → withholding in the payments section of Form 1040
- Form 1099-R Box 7 (distribution code) → identifies the type of distribution; may trigger a penalty
- Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox (next to Form 1099-R Box 7) → checked = Form 1040 Line 4; unchecked = Form 1040 Line 5
## Notes
- The "a" line (Form 1040 Line 4a / 5a) is the gross amount from Form 1099-R Box 1; the "b" line (Form 1040 Line 4b / 5b) is the taxable amount from Form 1099-R Box 2a
- Traditional IRA and 401(k) withdrawals are usually 100% taxable; if after-tax contributions were made, part is tax-free (Simplified Method or General Rule), so Form 1099-R Box 2a is less than Form 1099-R Box 1
- If Form 1099-R Box 2a is blank and "taxable amount not determined" (Form 1099-R Box 2b) is checked, the taxable amount must be figured separately
- Form 1099-R Box 7 distribution codes matter: an early distribution (before age 59.5) may trigger a 10% additional tax on Form 5329 / Schedule 2
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin at age 73 (age 75 for those born in 1960 or later) under SECURE 2.0; missing an RMD triggers an excise tax
## Required Information
- Payer name and TIN
- Gross distribution (Form 1099-R Box 1)
- Taxable amount (Form 1099-R Box 2a) and whether "not determined" (Form 1099-R Box 2b) is checked
- Distribution code (Form 1099-R Box 7)
- Whether the Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox is checked
- Federal income tax withheld (Form 1099-R Box 4)
## Questions
- Is the Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox checked (IRA → Form 1040 Line 4) or not (pension/annuity → Form 1040 Line 5)?
- Did you make any after-tax contributions to the plan (so part may be tax-free)?
- What is the Form 1099-R Box 7 distribution code?
- Were you under age 59.5 when you took the distribution?
## Common Errors
- Reporting the gross amount (Form 1099-R Box 1) as taxable when part is tax-free (after-tax basis)
- Putting an IRA distribution on Form 1040 Line 5 (or a pension on Form 1040 Line 4) by ignoring the Form 1099-R IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox
- Missing the 10% early-distribution penalty for withdrawals before age 59.5
- Missing a Required Minimum Distribution
## Prompt
- I took money out of my 401(k) this year and got a 1099-R.
- I receive a pension and got a 1099-R.
- I took an IRA distribution and received a 1099-R.
- I rolled over my retirement account and got a 1099-R.
