# Form 1099-R
## Description
Distributions from pensions, annuities, retirement or profit-sharing plans, IRAs, and insurance contracts.
## Which line does it go on?
The IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox (next to Box 7) decides whether the distribution is reported on Line 4 (IRAs) or Line 5 (pensions and annuities):
- IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box CHECKED → it is an IRA-type distribution → use Line 4
- IRA/SEP/SIMPLE box NOT checked → it is a pension, annuity, or employer plan (401(k), 403(b), etc.) → use Line 5
## Box-to-line mapping
- Box 1 (gross distribution) → Line 4a (if IRA) or Line 5a (if pension/annuity)
- Box 2a (taxable amount) → Line 4b (if IRA) or Line 5b (if pension/annuity)
- Box 4 (federal income tax withheld) → withholding in the payments section of Form 1040
- Box 7 (distribution code) → identifies the type of distribution; may trigger a penalty
- IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox (next to Box 7) → checked = Line 4; unchecked = Line 5
## Notes
- The "a" line is the gross amount (Box 1); the "b" line is the taxable amount (Box 2a)
- Traditional IRA and 401(k) withdrawals are usually 100% taxable; if after-tax contributions were made, part is tax-free (Simplified Method or General Rule), so Box 2a is less than Box 1
- If Box 2a is blank and "taxable amount not determined" (Box 2b) is checked, the taxable amount must be figured separately
- Box 7 distribution codes matter: an early distribution (before age 59.5) may trigger a 10% additional tax on Form 5329 / Schedule 2
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin at age 73 (age 75 for those born in 1960 or later) under SECURE 2.0; missing an RMD triggers an excise tax
## Required Information
- Payer name and TIN
- Gross distribution (Box 1)
- Taxable amount (Box 2a) and whether Box 2b "not determined" is checked
- Distribution code (Box 7)
- Whether the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox is checked
- Federal income tax withheld (Box 4)
## Questions
- Is the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox checked (IRA, Line 4) or not (pension/annuity, Line 5)?
- Did you make any after-tax contributions to the plan (so part may be tax-free)?
- What is the Box 7 distribution code?
- Were you under age 59.5 when you took the distribution?
## Common Errors
- Reporting the gross amount as taxable when part is tax-free (after-tax basis)
- Putting an IRA distribution on Line 5 (or a pension on Line 4) by ignoring the IRA/SEP/SIMPLE checkbox
- Missing the 10% early-distribution penalty for withdrawals before age 59.5
- Missing a Required Minimum Distribution
## Prompt
- I took money out of my 401(k) this year and got a 1099-R.
- I receive a pension and got a 1099-R.
- I took an IRA distribution and received a 1099-R.
- I rolled over my retirement account and got a 1099-R.
