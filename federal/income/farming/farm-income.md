# Schedule F — Profit or Loss From Farming
## Description
Reports income and expenses from operating a farm. Net profit or loss on Schedule F Line 34 flows to Schedule 1 Line 6 and to Schedule SE for self-employment tax.
## Key lines
- Schedule F Line 9: gross farm income
- Schedule F Line 33: total expenses
- Schedule F Line 34: NET FARM PROFIT OR LOSS — the number that goes to Schedule 1 Line 6 and Schedule SE
## Cash vs. accrual
- Most farmers use the CASH method: Parts I and II
- Accrual-method farmers complete Parts II and III, plus Part I Line 9
- Farmers get several timing elections unavailable to other businesses
## Farm-specific income items
- Sales of livestock and produce raised on the farm
- Sales of livestock and other items BOUGHT for resale (cost is subtracted separately)
- Cooperative distributions — Form 1099-PATR
- Agricultural program payments — often Form 1099-G
- Commodity Credit Corporation (CCC) loans reported under election
- Crop insurance proceeds and federal disaster payments — a deferral election to the following year may be available
- Custom hire (machine work) income
- Federal or state gasoline/fuel tax credit or refund
## Special elections
- WEATHER-RELATED LIVESTOCK SALES: if drought, flood, or other weather forced the sale, you may elect to report the income in the FOLLOWING year
- CROP INSURANCE: proceeds may be deferred one year if you normally report the crop income in the year after harvest
- INCOME AVERAGING: farmers may elect on Schedule J to average income over the prior three years
## What does NOT go on Schedule F
- Sale of farm MACHINERY, BREEDING LIVESTOCK, or LAND → Form 4797, not Schedule F
- Cash rent for farmland where you do not materially participate → Schedule E
- Crop-share rent without material participation → Form 4835
- Depreciation recapture on listed property whose business use dropped to 50% or less → Form 4797 Part IV
## Loss limitations (applied in order)
1. At-risk rules — Form 6198
2. Passive activity loss rules — Form 8582, if you do not materially participate
3. Excess business loss limitation — Form 461. Thresholds for 2026 are $256,000 (single, head of household, AND married filing separately) and $512,000 (married filing jointly and qualifying surviving spouse). These DROPPED from the 2025 amounts of $313,000 / $626,000: the One Big Beautiful Bill Act made the limitation permanent and reset the inflation-indexing base year to 2024, erasing several years of indexing. Married filing separately uses the SINGLE threshold, not half of the joint amount. Disallowed amounts carry forward as an NOL
## Required Information
- Gross receipts by category (livestock, produce, cooperative distributions, program payments)
- Cost of livestock and items bought for resale
- Farm expenses (feed, seed, fertilizer, chemicals, labor, repairs, depreciation, interest, rent)
- Whether you materially participated
- Any weather-related sale or crop insurance deferral elections
## Questions
- Do you operate a farm and materially participate in it?
- Do you use the cash or accrual method?
- Did you receive cooperative distributions (Form 1099-PATR) or agricultural program payments?
- Did weather force any livestock sales, or did you receive crop insurance?
- Did you sell farm equipment, breeding stock, or land (Form 4797 instead)?
## Common Errors
- Reporting sales of farm equipment or breeding livestock on Schedule F instead of Form 4797
- Reporting cash farmland rent on Schedule F when you did not materially participate
- Forgetting that Schedule F net profit is subject to self-employment tax
- Missing the weather-related livestock or crop insurance deferral elections
- Overlooking farm income as QBI-eligible
## Prompt
- I farm and need to report my crop and livestock income.
- I received a 1099-PATR from my co-op.
- I got crop insurance money after a drought.
