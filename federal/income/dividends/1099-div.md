# Form 1099-DIV
## Description
Dividend income reporting document from stocks and mutual funds. Individual boxes are detailed in the files below.
## Boxes
- Box 1a — Total ordinary dividends (ordinary-income/)
- Box 1b — Qualified dividends (ordinary-income/)
- Box 2a — Total capital gain distributions (capital-gains/)
- Box 5 — Section 199A dividends (qbi/)
## Notes
- Schedule B, Part II is required when total ordinary dividends exceed $1,500. At or below that, the amount goes directly on Form 1040 Line 3b
- Schedule B Part III also asks about foreign accounts and trusts, which can trigger FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR) reporting
- Qualified dividends (Box 1b) are a SUBSET of ordinary dividends (Box 1a), not an addition — Box 1b is never larger than Box 1a
## Questions
- Did you receive dividends from stocks or mutual funds?
- Do your total ordinary dividends exceed $1,500 (requiring Schedule B)?
- Were any of your dividends qualified dividends?
- Did any distributions come from REITs or mutual funds?
## Common Errors
- Missing a 1099-DIV
- Confusing ordinary and qualified dividends, or adding Box 1b to Box 1a
- Forgetting Schedule B when dividends exceed $1,500
## Prompt
- I received a 1099-DIV for dividends from my investments.
- My brokerage sent me a 1099-DIV with several boxes filled in.
