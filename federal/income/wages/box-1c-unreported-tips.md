# Form 1040 — Line 1c: Unreported Tip Income
## Description
Tip income that was not reported to your employer and therefore is not included in W-2 Box 1. Common for servers, bartenders, and other tipped employees who received cash tips.
## Notes
- This is EMPLOYEE income (earned as someone's employee), which is why it belongs on Line 1 rather than Line 8
- Reported through Form 4137, which also calculates the Social Security and Medicare tax you owe on these tips
- Cash tips are taxable even if the customer paid in cash and nothing was withheld
## Required Information
- Employer name
- Total amount of tips not reported to the employer
- Months in which the tips were received
## Questions
- Did you receive cash tips that you did not report to your employer?
- Are you a tipped employee (server, bartender, hairstylist, etc.)?
- Do you have records of your cash tips?
## Common Errors
- Not reporting cash tips because they were paid in cash
- Confusing this with self-employment income (tips as an employee go on Line 1c, not Schedule C)
- Forgetting Form 4137 for the Social Security and Medicare tax
## Prompt
- I earned cash tips at my server job that were not on my W-2.
- I received tips that I did not report to my employer.
- I am a bartender and got cash tips during the year.
