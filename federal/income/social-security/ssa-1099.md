# Form SSA-1099
## Description
Social Security Benefit Statement, reporting monthly retirement, survivor, and disability benefits paid by the Social Security Administration.
## Form 1040 Line 6a vs. Line 6b
- Form 1040 Line 6a = the TOTAL Social Security benefits you received (the full amount from SSA-1099 Box 5). This line itself is not taxed; it just shows the total.
- Form 1040 Line 6b = only the TAXABLE portion of those benefits, figured with a worksheet. This is the amount that actually counts as income and is taxed.
- So Line 6a is the whole benefit; Line 6b is the part (0% to 85%) that is subject to tax. Line 6b is always less than or equal to Line 6a.
## Box-to-line mapping
- SSA-1099 Box 5 (net benefits) → Form 1040 Line 6a
- Taxable portion (worksheet result) → Form 1040 Line 6b
- SSA-1099 Box 6 (voluntary federal income tax withheld) → Form 1040 Line 25b (tax already paid; credited on your return)
## How much is taxable
Whether any of the benefit is taxable depends on "combined income" (AGI + tax-exempt interest + half of Social Security benefits):
- Below the base amount ($25,000 single / $32,000 married filing jointly): none taxable (Form 1040 Line 6b = 0)
- Above the base amount: up to 50% taxable
- Above the upper amount ($34,000 single / $44,000 married filing jointly): up to 85% taxable
- Use the worksheet in the Form 1040 instructions or IRS Publication 915 to figure the Line 6b amount
## Notes
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is NOT reported here and is not taxable — do not confuse it with Social Security benefits
- Even when benefits are taxable, no more than 85% is ever taxed; Line 6b never exceeds 85% of Line 6a
- Married filing separately (lived with spouse) usually makes up to 85% taxable regardless of income
## Required Information
- Net benefits (SSA-1099 Box 5)
- Federal income tax withheld (SSA-1099 Box 6)
- Total of all other income (needed for the taxability worksheet)
## Questions
- Did you receive Social Security retirement, survivor, or disability benefits?
- What is your total other income (to determine how much of the benefit is taxable)?
- Did you have federal tax withheld from your benefits (SSA-1099 Box 6)?
## Common Errors
- Putting the taxable amount on Form 1040 Line 6a, or the total on Line 6b (6a = total, 6b = taxable)
- Assuming Social Security is never taxable (up to 85% can be)
- Assuming it is always fully taxable (never more than 85%)
- Confusing SSI (not taxable, not reported here) with Social Security benefits
- Forgetting to claim SSA-1099 Box 6 withholding on Form 1040 Line 25b
## Prompt
- I receive Social Security benefits and got an SSA-1099.
- My SSA-1099 shows my benefits for the year.
- I am retired and Social Security is my main income.
