# Form SSA-1099
## Description
Social Security Benefit Statement, reporting monthly retirement, survivor, and disability benefits paid by the Social Security Administration.
## Notes
- Report the net benefits from Box 5 on Line 6a; report the taxable portion on Line 6b
- Whether benefits are taxed depends on "combined income" (AGI + tax-exempt interest + half of Social Security benefits):
  - Below the base amount ($25,000 single / $32,000 married filing jointly): none taxable
  - Above the base amount: up to 50% taxable
  - Above the upper amount ($34,000 single / $44,000 married filing jointly): up to 85% taxable
- Use the worksheet in the Form 1040 instructions or Publication 915 to figure the taxable part
- Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is NOT reported here and is not taxable
- Federal tax withheld appears in Box 6 and goes in the payments section of Form 1040
## Required Information
- Net benefits (Box 5)
- Federal income tax withheld (Box 6)
- Total of all other income (needed for the taxability worksheet)
## Questions
- Did you receive Social Security retirement, survivor, or disability benefits?
- What is your total other income (to determine how much of the benefits is taxable)?
- Did you have federal tax withheld from your benefits (Box 6)?
## Common Errors
- Assuming Social Security is never taxable (up to 85% can be)
- Assuming it is always fully taxable
- Confusing SSI (not taxable, not reported here) with Social Security benefits
- Forgetting to claim Box 6 withholding as a payment
## Prompt
- I receive Social Security benefits and got an SSA-1099.
- My SSA-1099 shows my benefits for the year.
- I am retired and Social Security is my main income.
