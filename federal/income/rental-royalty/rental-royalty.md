# Rental and Royalty Income
## Description
Income from renting real estate and from royalties. Reported on Schedule E, which flows through Schedule 1, Line 5 to Form 1040, Line 8. This is generally passive income and is NOT subject to self-employment tax (unlike business income on Schedule C).
## Forms
- Form 1099-MISC — Box 1 rents and Box 2 royalties (1099-misc.md)
- Form 1099-K — Short-term rental income from platforms like Airbnb and VRBO (1099-k.md)
## Why rents and royalties are together
A royalty is not a kind of rent. They share this folder because both are reported in Schedule E, Part I, both flow through Schedule 1 Line 5, and both are generally passive. Royalties earned in a trade or business you actively operate are the exception — those go on Schedule C with self-employment tax. See 1099-misc.md
## Notes
- Rental income is offset by rental expenses (mortgage interest, property tax, repairs, depreciation) on Schedule E
- Passive losses may be limited; unused losses can carry forward
- Short-term rentals with substantial services (e.g., some Airbnb setups) can sometimes rise to Schedule C business treatment
## Questions
- Did you rent out real estate or receive royalties?
- Do you have records of rental income and expenses?
- Was any rental a short-term rental with hotel-like services?
## Prompt
- I have rental income from a property I own.
- I earned royalties this year.
- I rent out a property and received a 1099-MISC.
- I host on Airbnb and received a 1099-K.
