# Form 1099-K (Short-Term Rental)
## Description
Payment card and third-party network transactions from short-term rental platforms such as Airbnb and VRBO. This file covers 1099-K income from renting out property; for other 1099-K activity (rideshare, online selling, freelance), see business/1099-k.md.
## Notes
- Where this income goes depends on the LEVEL OF SERVICE provided, not the platform:
  - Renting a space with no substantial services (the common case) → Schedule E, passive rental income, generally NOT subject to self-employment tax
  - Providing substantial, hotel-like services (daily cleaning, meals, tours, concierge) → Schedule C, business income, subject to self-employment tax
- For tax year 2026 (and 2025 and beyond), platforms issue a 1099-K only when gross payments exceed $20,000 AND there are more than 200 transactions (One Big Beautiful Bill Act reverted the threshold to this pre-2022 level)
- Reports GROSS payments (before platform fees, cleaning fees paid out, refunds) — the taxable amount is net income after expenses
- All rental income is taxable even if no 1099-K was received
- Special rule: if you rent a home for fewer than 15 days in the year, the income is generally not taxable and is not reported (the "14-day" or Augusta rule)
## Required Information
- Filer/PSE name (the platform, e.g., Airbnb)
- Payer TIN
- Gross amount of payment transactions (Box 1a)
- Number of nights/transactions
- Rental expenses (cleaning, platform fees, mortgage interest, property tax, depreciation)
## Questions
- Did you rent out property through a platform like Airbnb or VRBO?
- Did you provide substantial services (cleaning during the stay, meals, tours), or just the space?
- Was the property rented fewer than 15 days this year?
- Do you have records of rental expenses to offset the gross amount?
## Common Errors
- Reporting the gross Box 1a amount instead of net income after expenses
- Putting passive rental income on Schedule C (or hotel-like service income on Schedule E)
- Failing to report rental income because no 1099-K was received
- Missing the under-15-days exclusion when it applies
## Prompt
- I host on Airbnb and received a 1099-K from the platform.
- I rent out my place on VRBO and got a 1099-K.
- I rent a room short-term and received a 1099-K.
