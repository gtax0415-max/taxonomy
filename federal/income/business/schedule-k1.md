# Schedule K-1
## Description
Reports a partner or S-corporation shareholder's share of income, deductions, and credits from a partnership (Form 1065) or S-corporation (Form 1120-S).
## Notes
- Each type of income on the K-1 flows to a different place: ordinary business income to Schedule E, interest/dividends/capital gains to their own schedules
- Partnership income may be subject to self-employment tax; S-corporation shareholder distributions generally are not
- May qualify for the QBI deduction
## Required Information
- Entity name and EIN
- Partner/shareholder identifying information
- Ordinary business income (loss)
- Separately stated items (interest, dividends, capital gains, Section 179, etc.)
## Questions
- Did you receive a Schedule K-1 from a partnership or S-corporation?
- Is this from a business you actively participate in, or a passive investment?
- Are there separately stated items that flow to other schedules?
## Common Errors
- Entering only the ordinary income and missing separately stated items
- Misclassifying passive vs. active participation
- Missing the QBI deduction on qualifying pass-through income
## Prompt
- I received a Schedule K-1 from a partnership I am part of.
- I got a K-1 from my S-corporation.
- I co-founded a company and received a K-1 for my share of the income.
