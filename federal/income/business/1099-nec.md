# Form 1099-NEC
## Description
Nonemployee compensation reporting document for independent contractors and freelancers.
## Notes
- Beginning with tax year 2026, payers issue a 1099-NEC only when payments to a non-employee total $2,000 or more in a calendar year (raised from $600 by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act). For payments in 2027 and later, this threshold is adjusted for inflation
- All income is taxable and must be reported even if no 1099-NEC was received; the threshold only controls whether a form is issued, not whether the income is taxable
- Net profit (after expenses on Schedule C) is generally subject to self-employment tax
## Required Information
- Payer name
- Payer TIN
- Nonemployee compensation (Box 1)
- Federal withholding
## Questions
- Did you receive nonemployee compensation as an independent contractor?
- Did you have business expenses to deduct against this income?
- Did you earn contractor income under $2,000 from any payer (which may not generate a form but is still taxable)?
## Common Errors
- Missing 1099-NEC
- Failing to report contractor income under the $2,000 threshold because no form was issued
- Forgetting to deduct business expenses on Schedule C
- Not paying self-employment tax
## Prompt
- I did freelance work for XYZ Corporation and received a 1099-NEC.
- I am an independent contractor and got a 1099-NEC for my services.
- I received nonemployee compensation for consulting work this year.
- I run a small business and several clients sent me 1099-NEC forms.
