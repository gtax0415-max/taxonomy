# Form 1099-MISC
## Description
Miscellaneous information return reporting rents, royalties, prizes and awards, and other income that is not nonemployee compensation.
## Notes
- Since 2020, nonemployee compensation moved to Form 1099-NEC; 1099-MISC now covers rents, royalties, and other miscellaneous payments
- Where the amount goes depends on which box is filled: rents/royalties usually flow through Schedule E, business income through Schedule C, and one-off amounts (prizes, awards) through Schedule 1 other income
## Required Information
- Payer name
- Payer TIN
- Rents (Box 1)
- Royalties (Box 2)
- Other income (Box 3)
- Federal income tax withheld (Box 4)
## Questions
- What type of payment did you receive (rent, royalty, prize, or other)?
- Is this connected to a trade or business you operate?
- Do you have related expenses to deduct?
## Common Errors
- Reporting nonemployee compensation here instead of on 1099-NEC
- Putting rental or royalty income on Schedule C instead of Schedule E
- Forgetting that prizes and awards are taxable other income
## Prompt
- I received a 1099-MISC for rent paid to me.
- I got a 1099-MISC for royalties on my creative work.
- My 1099-MISC shows an amount in Box 3 for a prize I won.
- I received miscellaneous income reported on a 1099-MISC.
