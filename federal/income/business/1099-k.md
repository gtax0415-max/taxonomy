# Form 1099-K
## Description
Payment card and third-party network transactions, reporting gross payments received through platforms such as PayPal, Venmo, Cash App, eBay, Etsy, Uber, and similar payment settlement entities. This file covers business/self-employment 1099-K income; for short-term rental income (Airbnb, VRBO), see rental/1099-k.md.
## Notes
- For tax year 2026 (and 2025 and beyond), platforms are required to issue a 1099-K only when gross payments exceed $20,000 AND there are more than 200 transactions. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 4, 2025) permanently reverted the threshold to this pre-2022 level, cancelling the previously planned $2,500 (2025) and $600 (2026) thresholds
- A lower threshold may still apply in some states, or when backup withholding occurred
- Reports GROSS payments (before fees, refunds, or cost of goods) — the taxable amount is the net profit, not the gross figure
- All income is taxable and must be reported even if no 1099-K was received; the threshold only controls whether a form is issued, not whether the income is taxable
- Short-term rental income (Airbnb, VRBO) is handled separately — see rental/1099-k.md — because it usually flows to Schedule E (passive rental) rather than Schedule C
## Required Information
- Filer/PSE name (the platform)
- Payer TIN
- Gross amount of payment transactions (Box 1a)
- Number of payment transactions
- Federal income tax withheld (backup withholding)
## Questions
- Did you receive payments for goods or services through a payment app or online platform?
- Was the activity a business/self-employment, or the casual sale of personal items?
- Do you have records of fees, refunds, and cost of goods to reduce the gross amount to net profit?
- Were any of the payments personal (gifts, reimbursements, splitting a bill) rather than income?
## Common Errors
- Reporting the gross Box 1a amount as taxable income instead of the net profit
- Failing to report income because no 1099-K was received (the reporting threshold is high, but the income is still taxable)
- Mixing personal transfers (splitting a bill, gifts) with business income
- Double-counting income also reported on a 1099-NEC or 1099-MISC from the same activity
## Prompt
- I sell items on eBay and received a 1099-K.
- My payment app sent me a 1099-K for payments I received this year.
- I drive for a rideshare company and got a 1099-K.
- I received a 1099-K for online sales this year.
