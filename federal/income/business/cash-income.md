# Cash and Unreported Self-Employment Income
## Description
Self-employment income received in cash or otherwise not reported on a 1099-NEC, 1099-K, or 1099-MISC. This is income earned working for yourself (freelance gigs, side business, cash jobs), and it is fully taxable.
## Notes
- This is SELF-EMPLOYMENT income (working for yourself), so it goes on Schedule C → Line 8, and the net profit is subject to self-employment tax
- If instead you earned this as someone's employee (e.g., unreported cash tips or wages), it belongs on Form 1040, Line 1 (see the wages folder), NOT here
- Report all gross receipts on Schedule C, Line 1 — including cash gigs and small jobs where no form was issued
- No 1099 does not mean tax-free
## Required Information
- Description of the business or activity
- Total gross receipts (including amounts with no 1099)
- Related business expenses
## Questions
- Did you earn self-employment income in cash or with no 1099?
- Were you working for yourself (not as an employee) for this income?
- Do you have records of the receipts and any business expenses?
## Common Errors
- Omitting cash income because no form was issued
- Reporting employee cash tips/wages here instead of on Line 1
- Failing to keep records of cash receipts
- Forgetting self-employment tax on the net profit
## Prompt
- I earned money from cash gigs this year with no 1099.
- I got paid in cash for freelance side jobs.
- I have self-employment income that was not reported on any form.
