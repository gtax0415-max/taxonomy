# Form 1099-INT
## Description
Interest income reporting document from banks and financial institutions. Individual boxes are detailed in the files below.
## Boxes
- Box 1 — Interest income → Form 1040, Line 2b (taxable/)
- Box 3 — U.S. savings bonds and Treasury interest → Form 1040, Line 2b, state-exempt (taxable/)
- Box 8 — Tax-exempt interest → Form 1040, Line 2a (tax-exempt/)
- Box 9 — Specified private activity bond interest → Form 1040, Line 2a + Form 6251 AMT (tax-exempt/)
## Other boxes that route elsewhere
- Box 2 — Early withdrawal penalty → a DEDUCTION on Schedule 1, Line 18. See federal/deductions/adjustments/early-withdrawal-penalty.md
- Box 4 — Federal income tax withheld → Form 1040, Line 25b
- Box 6 — Foreign tax paid → may support a foreign tax credit on Form 1116
## When Schedule B is required
- Schedule B, PART I is required when total TAXABLE interest exceeds $1,500. At or below that, the amount goes directly on Form 1040 Line 2b
- The $1,500 threshold is based on taxable interest only. Tax-exempt interest (Box 8) goes on Line 2a and does not count toward the threshold
- The same $1,500 threshold applies separately to ordinary dividends in Schedule B Part II — see federal/income/dividends/1099-div.md
- Schedule B is ALSO required regardless of amount if you received interest as a nominee, accrued interest on bonds, or excluded savings bond interest under an education exclusion
- Schedule B, PART III asks about foreign bank accounts and foreign trusts. Answering yes can trigger FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR) and Form 8938 reporting, which carry substantial penalties if missed
## Notes
- The $1,500 threshold is not indexed for inflation and has been unchanged for many years
- Interest is taxable even when it is left in the account and not withdrawn
- Banks issue a 1099-INT at $10 or more, but interest below that is still taxable and must be reported
## Questions
- Did you receive interest from a bank or financial institution?
- Was your total taxable interest over $1,500 (requiring Schedule B Part I)?
- Did you have any tax-exempt or municipal bond interest?
- Do you have any foreign bank or financial accounts (Schedule B Part III)?
- Did you cash in a CD early and pay a penalty (Box 2)?
## Common Errors
- Forgetting Schedule B when taxable interest exceeds $1,500
- Counting tax-exempt interest toward the $1,500 threshold
- Omitting interest under $10 because no 1099-INT arrived
- Missing the Box 2 early withdrawal penalty deduction
- Answering the Schedule B Part III foreign account questions incorrectly
## Prompt
- I received a 1099-INT from my bank.
- My 1099-INT has several boxes filled in, including tax-exempt interest.
- I earned interest on my savings account this year.
