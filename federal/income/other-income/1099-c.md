# Form 1099-C — Cancellation of Debt
## Description
Reports debt that a lender canceled, forgave, or discharged. Forgiven debt is generally TAXABLE INCOME because you received something of value without having to repay it.
## Box-to-line mapping
- Form 1099-C Box 2 (amount of debt discharged) → Schedule 1 Line 8c → Form 1040 Line 8
- Form 1099-C Box 3: interest included in Box 2
- Form 1099-C Box 6: identifiable event code explaining why the debt was discharged
- Form 1099-C Box 7: fair market value of any property involved (relevant for foreclosures)
## Exclusions — when canceled debt is NOT taxable
File Form 982 to claim an exclusion. The main ones:
- BANKRUPTCY: debt discharged in a Title 11 case is excluded
- INSOLVENCY: excluded to the extent your liabilities exceeded the fair market value of your assets IMMEDIATELY BEFORE the discharge. This is the most commonly used exclusion and requires a careful solvency worksheet
- QUALIFIED PRINCIPAL RESIDENCE INDEBTEDNESS: mortgage debt on a main home, within limits
- QUALIFIED FARM INDEBTEDNESS
- QUALIFIED REAL PROPERTY BUSINESS INDEBTEDNESS
- Certain STUDENT LOAN discharges, including death or total and permanent disability discharges, and discharges under income-driven repayment programs during periods Congress has excluded them
- Gifts — a debt forgiven out of natural love and affection is a gift rather than income
## Notes
- Receiving a Form 1099-C does not automatically mean the amount is taxable; it means you must report it and then claim any exclusion
- Claiming an exclusion generally requires REDUCING TAX ATTRIBUTES (net operating losses, credits, basis in property) under Form 982
- Debt canceled in a FORECLOSURE can produce BOTH cancellation of debt income AND a gain or loss on the disposition of the property
- Business debt canceled is reported on the business schedule rather than Schedule 1 Line 8c
## Required Information
- Form 1099-C, all boxes
- Total assets and total liabilities immediately before the discharge (for the insolvency test)
- Bankruptcy documentation if applicable
- Property basis and fair market value if a foreclosure was involved
## Questions
- Did a lender cancel, forgive, or settle a debt for less than you owed?
- Were you insolvent immediately before the cancellation (liabilities exceeding assets)?
- Was the debt discharged in bankruptcy?
- Was it mortgage debt on your main home, or a student loan?
- Was property foreclosed on or repossessed?
## Common Errors
- Ignoring a Form 1099-C and omitting the income entirely
- Assuming all canceled debt is taxable and failing to claim the insolvency or bankruptcy exclusion
- Claiming an exclusion without filing Form 982 or reducing tax attributes
- Missing the separate gain or loss on the property in a foreclosure
## Prompt
- A credit card company forgave part of my balance and sent me a 1099-C.
- I settled a debt for less than I owed.
- My home was foreclosed on and I received a 1099-C.
