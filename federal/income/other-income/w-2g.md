# Form W-2G
## Description
Certain gambling winnings — from casinos, lotteries, raffles, poker tournaments, and similar — reported when winnings reach the reporting threshold for that type of game.
## Notes
- Gambling winnings are fully taxable and go on Schedule 1, Line 8b, then to Form 1040, Line 8
- All gambling winnings are taxable even if below the threshold for a W-2G (the form is not always issued)
- Gambling LOSSES are deductible only if you itemize (Schedule A), and only up to the amount of your winnings — they never create a net loss
- Federal tax may have been withheld (Box 4) — claim it as a payment
## Required Information
- Payer name and TIN
- Gross winnings (Box 1)
- Type of wager
- Federal income tax withheld (Box 4)
## Questions
- Did you win money gambling (casino, lottery, poker, raffle)?
- Do you have records of your gambling losses (for a possible itemized deduction)?
- Was federal tax withheld from your winnings?
## Common Errors
- Not reporting winnings below the W-2G threshold (still taxable)
- Trying to deduct gambling losses without itemizing, or beyond the amount of winnings
- Netting losses against winnings on the income side (winnings go in full on Line 8b; losses only on Schedule A)
- Forgetting to claim Box 4 withholding as a payment
## Prompt
- I won money at a casino and received a W-2G.
- I hit a lottery / scratch-off and got a W-2G.
- I won a poker tournament and received a W-2G.
