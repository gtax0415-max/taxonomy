# Form W-2G
## Description
Certain gambling winnings — from casinos, lotteries, raffles, poker tournaments, and similar — reported when winnings reach the reporting threshold for that type of game.
## Box-to-line mapping
- Form W-2G Box 1 (reportable winnings) → Schedule 1 Line 8b → Form 1040 Line 8
- Form W-2G Box 4 (federal income tax withheld) → Form 1040 Line 25b (tax already paid; credited on your return)
- Form W-2G Box 2 (date won), Box 3 (type of wager), Box 14 (state tax withheld) → supporting detail; state withholding goes on the state return
## Winnings vs. losses
- WINNINGS: reported on Form W-2G Box 1 and entered in full on Schedule 1 Line 8b. All gambling winnings are taxable even when no Form W-2G was issued (the form is only required above certain thresholds by game type)
- LOSSES: there is NO tax form for gambling losses — casinos and lotteries do not issue a 1099 or any other form for what you lost. You must substantiate losses with your own records: a gambling log/diary (date, location, game, amounts won and lost), player's club or win/loss statements from the casino, tickets, receipts, and bank/ATM records
- Losses are deductible ONLY as an itemized deduction on Schedule A, and ONLY up to the amount of winnings reported as income. They can never create a net loss or offset other income
- If you take the standard deduction, gambling losses give you no tax benefit at all — but the winnings are still fully taxable
## Notes
- Winnings go in FULL on the income side; you cannot subtract losses before reporting (no netting)
- A casino "win/loss statement" is a summary, not an official tax form, and is generally not sufficient on its own — keep your own contemporaneous records
- Professional gamblers are treated differently (Schedule C business), but that is a high bar and not the usual case
## Required Information
- Payer name and TIN
- Reportable winnings (Form W-2G Box 1)
- Type of wager (Form W-2G Box 3)
- Federal income tax withheld (Form W-2G Box 4)
- Your own records of gambling losses (no form is issued for these)
## Questions
- Did you win money gambling (casino, lottery, poker, raffle)?
- Did you receive one or more Forms W-2G, or did you win amounts below the reporting threshold?
- Do you have records of your gambling losses (log, win/loss statement, receipts)?
- Will you itemize deductions (required to deduct any losses)?
- Was federal tax withheld (Form W-2G Box 4)?
## Common Errors
- Not reporting winnings below the Form W-2G threshold (still fully taxable)
- Netting losses against winnings on the income side (winnings go in full on Schedule 1 Line 8b; losses only on Schedule A)
- Trying to deduct gambling losses without itemizing, or beyond the amount of winnings
- Expecting a form for losses — none exists; losses require your own documentation
- Forgetting to claim Form W-2G Box 4 withholding on Form 1040 Line 25b
## Prompt
- I won money at a casino and received a W-2G.
- I hit a lottery / scratch-off and got a W-2G.
- I won a poker tournament and received a W-2G.
- I had gambling winnings and also significant losses this year.
