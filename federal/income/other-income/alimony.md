# Alimony Received
## Description
Spousal support (alimony or separate maintenance) received from a former spouse. Whether it is taxable depends entirely on the date of the divorce or separation agreement.
## Notes
- The dividing line is December 31, 2018 (from the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act):
  - Agreements executed ON OR BEFORE December 31, 2018: alimony received IS taxable income to the recipient. Report on Schedule 1, Line 2a, and enter the date of the original agreement on Line 2b
  - Agreements executed ON OR AFTER January 1, 2019: alimony is NOT taxable and is NOT reported by the recipient
- The rule follows the date the agreement was executed, not when payments are made
- A pre-2019 agreement modified after 2018 keeps the old rules UNLESS the modification expressly states the new (TCJA) rules apply
- This file is the recipient side (income). The payer side is a deduction, not income
- Child support is never taxable and is separate from alimony
## Required Information
- Date the divorce or separation agreement was executed
- Amount of alimony received
- Whether the agreement was modified after 2018 (and what the modification said)
## Questions
- When was your divorce or separation agreement finalized (before 2019, or 2019 and later)?
- Are you receiving alimony/spousal support (as opposed to child support)?
- Was the agreement modified after 2018?
## Common Errors
- Reporting post-2018 alimony as taxable income (it is not)
- Failing to report pre-2019 alimony that is taxable
- Confusing child support (never taxable) with alimony
- Missing the agreement date on Line 2b for pre-2019 alimony
## Prompt
- I receive alimony from my ex-spouse and my divorce was finalized before 2019.
- I get spousal support payments from a divorce settled in 2022.
- I receive alimony and am not sure if I need to report it.
