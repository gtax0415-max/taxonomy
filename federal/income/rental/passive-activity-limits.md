# Passive Activity Loss Limitations (Form 8582)
## Description
Limits how much loss from passive activities — most rental real estate and any business you do not materially participate in — you can deduct against other income. About 8.1 million returns file Form 8582 (IRS SOI, TY 2022), making it one of the most common limitation forms.
## Note on sources
Schedule E and the current-year Form 8582 are COMPUTED on the return, so neither is a source document. The sources are the underlying rental records and, importantly, the PRIOR-YEAR Form 8582, which is the only record of suspended losses carried forward. Keep prior-year Forms 8582 indefinitely for the same reason you keep prior-year Forms 8606 for IRA basis.
## The general rule
Passive losses can only offset PASSIVE INCOME. They cannot offset wages, interest, dividends, or active business income. Disallowed losses are SUSPENDED and carried forward indefinitely.
## Rental real estate is passive by default
Rental activity is treated as passive REGARDLESS of how much you participate — with the exceptions below. This surprises many landlords who assume rental losses freely offset their salary.
## Exception 1: the $25,000 special allowance
If you ACTIVELY PARTICIPATE in rental real estate (a lower bar than material participation — approving tenants, setting rents, approving repairs), you may deduct up to $25,000 of rental loss against nonpassive income.
- Phases out at 50 cents per dollar once MAGI exceeds $100,000
- Completely gone at MAGI of $150,000
- The $25,000 and the phase-out range are NOT indexed for inflation, so they have eroded over time
- NOT DOUBLED for joint filers: a single filer and a married couple filing jointly both get the same $25,000, and the same $100,000 phase-out threshold. Two unmarried co-owners could each claim up to $25,000; once married they share one. This is the same marriage-penalty pattern as the SALT cap
- Married filing separately: reduced to $12,500, and only if you lived apart from your spouse for the ENTIRE year; $0 if you lived together at any point. The phase-out threshold is also halved to $50,000
- These amounts are set by statute and have NOT been indexed since 1986, so inflation has steadily eroded the benefit. They are the same for 2026 as for prior years
## Exception 2: real estate professional
If you meet BOTH tests, your rental activities are not automatically passive:
1. More than half of your personal services during the year are in real property trades or businesses, AND
2. More than 750 hours of service in those activities
You must also materially participate in the rental activity itself. Contemporaneous time logs are essential — this is a frequently audited area.
## Exception 3: short-term rentals
Average guest stays of seven days or fewer are not automatically "rental activity" under the passive rules. With material participation, such an activity can be nonpassive — relevant to some Airbnb and VRBO operations. See federal/income/rental/1099-k.md
## When suspended losses are released
Suspended passive losses are freed when you DISPOSE OF THE ENTIRE ACTIVITY in a fully taxable transaction to an unrelated party. At that point the accumulated losses become deductible in full.
## Notes
- The at-risk rules (Form 6198) are applied BEFORE the passive activity rules
- The excess business loss limitation (Form 461) applies after both
- Passive income from one activity can absorb passive losses from another
## Required Information
- Rental income and expenses by property (from the underlying records, not from Schedule E)
- Hours of participation and the nature of participation
- MAGI (for the $25,000 phase-out)
- Suspended losses carried forward, from the prior-year Form 8582
## Questions
- Do you have a rental loss, and what is your MAGI?
- Do you actively participate (approving tenants, setting rents, approving expenditures)?
- Do you qualify as a real estate professional (750 hours and more than half your working time)?
- Are your rentals short-term with an average stay of seven days or fewer?
- Do you have suspended losses from prior years (check the prior-year Form 8582)?
- Did you sell an entire rental activity this year?
## Common Errors
- Assuming rental losses can freely offset wages
- Missing the $25,000 special allowance by not documenting active participation
- Claiming real estate professional status without contemporaneous time records
- Losing prior-year Forms 8582 and thereby losing the record of suspended losses
- Forgetting to release suspended losses when the activity is finally sold
- Overlooking the MAGI phase-out between $100,000 and $150,000
