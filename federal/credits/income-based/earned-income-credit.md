# Earned Income Credit (EIC)
## Description
Fully refundable credit for working people with low to moderate income. Because it is refundable, it can produce a refund far larger than any tax withheld. Schedule EIC appears on about 17.2 million returns (IRS SOI, TY 2022).
## How it works
- Based on EARNED INCOME (wages, self-employment income) and the number of qualifying children
- The credit rises with earned income, plateaus, then phases out. Both earned income AND AGI must be under the limit
- Larger families receive substantially more; the credit is available with zero, one, two, or three or more qualifying children
- Amounts and income limits are indexed annually — confirm the current-year table before filing
## Key eligibility rules
- You, your spouse, and any qualifying children must have valid SSNs issued by the return due date
- You must have earned income; investment income above an annual limit disqualifies you entirely
- You cannot file MARRIED FILING SEPARATELY, with a narrow exception for certain separated spouses living apart
- You must be a U.S. citizen or resident alien all year
- You cannot be a qualifying child of another person
- Without a qualifying child, age limits apply (generally at least 25 and under 65)
- You cannot claim it if you file Form 2555 (foreign earned income exclusion)
## Qualifying child tests
Relationship, age, residency (lived with you in the U.S. more than half the year), and joint return tests. A qualifying child for the EIC does not have to be your dependent in every case, and the age limit is more generous than for the Child Tax Credit — under 19, under 24 if a full-time student, or any age if permanently and totally disabled.
## Notes
- Refunds that include the EIC cannot be issued before mid-February by law, which delays some returns
- Improper EIC claims carry a penalty regime: a taxpayer who claims it recklessly or fraudulently can be BARRED from claiming it for 2 to 10 years and must then file Form 8862 to reclaim it
- Combat pay can be included or excluded from earned income at your election, whichever produces the larger credit
## Required Information
- Total earned income (Form W-2 Box 1, self-employment net earnings)
- AGI and investment income
- Each qualifying child's name, SSN, date of birth, relationship, and months lived with you
- Filing status
## Questions
- What was your earned income and AGI for the year?
- Do you have qualifying children, and do they have valid SSNs?
- Is your investment income under the annual limit?
- Are you filing married filing separately (which usually disqualifies you)?
- Were you previously barred from claiming the EIC (Form 8862 may be required)?
## Common Errors
- Claiming a child who did not live with you more than half the year in the United States
- Two people claiming the same child without applying the tiebreaker rules
- Overlooking investment income that exceeds the limit
- Claiming it while married filing separately
- Missing Form 8862 after a prior disallowance
## Prompt
- Do I qualify for the earned income credit?
- I work part-time and have two kids.
- I heard I might get money back even though I owe no tax.
