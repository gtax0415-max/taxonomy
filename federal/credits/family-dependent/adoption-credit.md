# Adoption Credit
## Description
Credit for qualified expenses of adopting an eligible child. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act made part of it REFUNDABLE beginning in 2025, a significant change from its long history as a purely nonrefundable credit.
## Amounts
- Maximum credit: $17,280 of qualified expenses for 2025, indexed annually
- Beginning in 2025, up to $5,000 of the credit is REFUNDABLE. The rest remains nonrefundable and can be carried forward up to five years
- Phase-out begins at modified AGI above $259,190 (2025) and is complete at $40,000 above that
- SPECIAL NEEDS ADOPTION: if a state determines the child has special needs, you may claim the FULL credit even if your actual expenses were lower or zero
## Qualified expenses
- Adoption fees, attorney fees, and court costs
- Travel expenses including meals and lodging while away from home
- Re-adoption expenses for a foreign child
## What does NOT qualify
- Adopting your spouse's child (stepchild adoption)
- A surrogate parenting arrangement
- Expenses reimbursed by an employer or a government program
- Expenses that violate state or federal law
## Timing rules
- DOMESTIC adoption: expenses paid BEFORE the adoption is final are claimed in the year AFTER they are paid. Expenses paid in the year the adoption becomes final, or later, are claimed in the year paid
- FOREIGN adoption: no credit is allowed until the adoption becomes FINAL. All prior-year expenses are claimed in the year of finalization
- This timing difference catches many filers by surprise
## Required Information
- Each child's name, year of birth, and identifying number
- Whether the child has special needs and the state determination
- Qualified expenses by year paid
- Whether the adoption is domestic or foreign, and the finalization date
- Any employer adoption assistance received (a separate exclusion)
## Questions
- Did you adopt or begin adopting a child?
- Is the adoption domestic or foreign, and is it final?
- Did a state determine the child has special needs?
- Did your employer provide adoption assistance?
- Is your modified AGI near the phase-out range?
## Common Errors
- Claiming pre-finalization expenses too early in a domestic adoption, or claiming any expenses before finalization in a foreign adoption
- Claiming a stepchild adoption
- Missing the full credit available for a special needs adoption regardless of actual expenses
- Overlooking the new refundable portion available from 2025
- Double-counting expenses also excluded under an employer adoption assistance program
## Prompt
- We adopted a child this year.
- I am in the process of adopting and paid attorney fees.
- We adopted a child with special needs.
