# Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116)
## Description
Nonrefundable credit for income tax paid or accrued to a foreign country or U.S. possession, preventing double taxation of the same income.
## The simplified election — the case most people are in
You may claim the credit DIRECTLY on Schedule 3, Line 1 without filing Form 1116 if ALL of these are true:
- Total creditable foreign tax is $300 or less ($600 or less on a joint return)
- ALL the foreign income is PASSIVE — interest, dividends, royalties
- All of it was reported on a payee statement such as Form 1099-DIV Box 7 or Form 1099-INT Box 6
- You elect not to carry any unused foreign tax back or forward
This covers most people whose only foreign tax is withholding on an international mutual fund or ADR.
## Credit or deduction — you choose
Foreign taxes can be taken EITHER as this credit OR as an itemized deduction on Schedule A, but not both in the same year. The credit is usually worth more because it reduces tax dollar for dollar, while a deduction only reduces taxable income. The deduction can occasionally win if the credit is heavily limited.
## The limitation
The credit cannot exceed the U.S. tax attributable to your foreign-source income. In effect it is capped at the U.S. tax you would have paid on that income, so a foreign country taxing at a higher rate than the U.S. leaves you with unused credit.
- Unused foreign tax carries BACK 1 year and FORWARD 10 years
- The limitation is computed SEPARATELY for each income category (passive, general, and others), so credits in one basket cannot offset another
## Foreign earned income exclusion interaction
If you exclude foreign wages under Form 2555, you cannot also claim a credit for foreign tax paid on the EXCLUDED income. Running both requires allocating between excluded and non-excluded amounts, and the exclusion also disqualifies you from the Earned Income Credit.
## Required Information
- Form 1099-DIV Box 7 and Form 1099-INT Box 6 amounts
- Foreign taxes from Schedule K-1
- Foreign source income by category
- Date paid or accrued and the currency conversion used
- Any carryback or carryforward from other years
## Questions
- How much foreign tax was paid or withheld, and is it at or under $300 ($600 joint)?
- Is all the foreign income passive and reported on payee statements?
- Do you have foreign wages, rental income, or business income (which requires Form 1116)?
- Are you also claiming the foreign earned income exclusion on Form 2555?
- Do you have unused foreign tax credit carryforwards?
## Common Errors
- Filing Form 1116 unnecessarily when the simplified election would apply
- Claiming both the credit and the Schedule A deduction for the same taxes
- Claiming a credit for foreign tax on income excluded under Form 2555
- Missing the separate limitation by income category
- Letting carryforwards expire unused after 10 years
## Prompt
- My 1099-DIV shows foreign tax paid in Box 7.
- I paid income tax to another country.
- I have foreign investments with tax withheld.
