# Qualified Tips Deduction (2025-2028)
## Description
Deduction of up to $25,000 for qualified tip income, created by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act for tax years 2025 through 2028. Commonly called "no tax on tips."
## Notes
- Maximum deduction: $25,000 PER RETURN — this amount is NOT doubled for married couples filing jointly. A married couple who both earn tips still share a single $25,000 cap, which is a marriage penalty
- MARRIED TAXPAYERS MUST FILE JOINTLY to claim this deduction. Married filing separately is completely barred
- Phases out when MAGI exceeds $150,000 ($300,000 married filing jointly) — the phase-out threshold IS doubled for joint filers, unlike the cap itself
- The deduction is reduced by $100 for each $1,000 of MAGI over the threshold
- A valid Social Security number for employment is required on the return
- Self-employed: the deduction cannot exceed net income from the trade or business in which the tips were earned. Self-employed individuals in a Specified Service Trade or Business (SSTB) under Section 199A are NOT eligible, and neither are employees whose employer is an SSTB
- Available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize
- The occupation must be one that customarily and regularly received tips (IRS publishes the list of qualifying occupations)
- Tips must be VOLUNTARY — mandatory service charges and auto-gratuities do not qualify
- Tips must be REPORTED to qualify for the deduction
- This is a DEDUCTION, not an exclusion: tips remain in Form W-2 Box 1 wages, flow to Form 1040 Line 1a as income, and remain subject to Social Security and Medicare (FICA) tax
- Reporting: for tax year 2026 and later, employers MUST report qualified tips in Form W-2 Box 12 using new code TP, and must enter the Treasury Tipped Occupation Code (TTOC) in the new Box 14b. The code TP amount is NOT excluded from Box 1 wages — it simply identifies the deductible portion
- For tax year 2025 there was no dedicated box; under Notice 2025-62 the IRS treated it as a transition period, so taxpayers used pay stubs, tip records, and safe-harbor methods

## Required Information
- Amount of qualified tips for the year
- Occupation (to confirm it is on the IRS tipped-occupation list)
- MAGI and filing status (for the phase-out)
## Questions
- Did you receive tip income as an employee?
- Is your occupation one that customarily receives tips?
- Were the tips voluntary (not mandatory service charges)?
- Is your MAGI above $150,000 ($300,000 joint)?
## Common Errors
- Believing tips are excluded from income (they are still wages; this is a separate deduction)
- Including mandatory service charges or auto-gratuities
- Claiming unreported tips (tips must be reported to qualify)
- Assuming FICA no longer applies
- Missing the MAGI phase-out
## Prompt
- I earn tips at my job and want to claim the new tips deduction.
- I am a server and heard tips are not taxed anymore.
