# Qualified Overtime Deduction (2025-2028)
## Description
Deduction for the premium portion of overtime pay, created by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act for tax years 2025 through 2028. Commonly called "no tax on overtime."
## Notes
- Maximum deduction: $12,500 ($25,000 married filing jointly) — unlike the tips deduction, this cap IS doubled for joint filers
- MARRIED TAXPAYERS MUST FILE JOINTLY to claim this deduction. Married filing separately is completely barred
- Phases out when MAGI exceeds $150,000 ($300,000 married filing jointly), reduced by $100 for each $1,000 over the threshold
- A valid Social Security number for employment is required on the return
- Available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize
- ONLY THE PREMIUM PORTION QUALIFIES — the extra "half" of time-and-a-half, not the entire overtime payment. Example: regular rate $25/hour, overtime rate $37.50/hour; only the $12.50/hour premium counts toward the deduction
- Applies to overtime required under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- This is a DEDUCTION, not an exclusion: overtime remains in Form W-2 Box 1 wages, flows to Form 1040 Line 1a as income, and remains subject to Social Security and Medicare (FICA) tax
- Reporting: for tax year 2026 and later, employers MUST report qualified overtime in Form W-2 Box 12 using new code TT. The code TT amount is NOT excluded from Box 1 wages — it is already in Box 1 and code TT simply identifies the deductible portion
- Only FLSA-REQUIRED overtime qualifies for code TT. State-law-only overtime (such as California daily overtime), union contract overtime above the FLSA requirement, voluntary employer premiums, and overtime paid to exempt employees do NOT qualify
- For tax year 2025 there was no dedicated box; Notice 2025-69 and the Schedule 1-A instructions provided calculation methods from pay stubs and payroll records
## Related
- Income side: see income/wages/box-1a-w2-wages.md (overtime included in Form W-2 Box 1, Form 1040 Line 1a)
## Required Information
- Total overtime compensation and the premium ("half") portion
- MAGI and filing status (for the phase-out and the cap)
## Questions
- Did you work overtime as an hourly employee covered by the FLSA?
- Do you know the premium portion of your overtime pay, separate from your regular rate?
- Is your MAGI above $150,000 ($300,000 joint)?
- What is your filing status (the cap doubles for joint filers)?
## Common Errors
- Deducting the FULL overtime pay instead of only the premium portion
- Believing overtime is excluded from income (it is still wages; this is a separate deduction)
- Assuming FICA no longer applies
- Missing the MAGI phase-out
- Using the $25,000 cap when not filing jointly
## Prompt
- I worked a lot of overtime this year and heard it is not taxed.
- I want to claim the new overtime deduction.
