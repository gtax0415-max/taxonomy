# Saver's Credit
## Description
Nonrefundable credit for contributing to a retirement account, formally the Credit for Qualified Retirement Savings Contributions. Claimed on Form 8880, filed on about 9.4 million returns (IRS SOI, TY 2022).
## How it works
- The credit is 50%, 20%, or 10% of up to $2,000 of contributions per person, depending on AGI and filing status
- Maximum credit is therefore $1,000 per person, or $2,000 on a joint return where both spouses contribute
- The AGI brackets DIFFER BY FILING STATUS in a fixed ratio: the married filing jointly brackets are exactly DOUBLE the single brackets, and head of household is 1.5 TIMES the single brackets. Married filing separately uses the single amounts
- The brackets are indexed annually — confirm the current-year table before filing
- NONREFUNDABLE: it can reduce tax to zero but produces no refund, which is why many low-income savers who would qualify on income get no benefit
## Qualifying contributions
- Traditional and Roth IRA contributions
- Elective deferrals to a 401(k), 403(b), governmental 457(b), SARSEP, or SIMPLE plan
- Voluntary after-tax employee contributions to a qualified plan
- ABLE account contributions by the designated beneficiary
## Who cannot claim it
- Anyone under age 18
- A full-time student
- Anyone claimed as a dependent on someone else's return
## The distribution offset — commonly missed
Contributions are REDUCED by any distributions you (or your spouse on a joint return) received from retirement plans during the year, the two prior years, and the period up to the filing deadline. Taking money out of an IRA while also contributing can wipe out the credit entirely.
## Notes
- This credit is IN ADDITION to the deduction for a traditional IRA or the pre-tax exclusion for a 401(k) deferral. The same contribution can generate both — see federal/deductions/adjustments/ira-deduction.md
- Roth contributions get no deduction but DO count for this credit
## Required Information
- Amount contributed to each account type
- Form W-2 Box 12 elective deferral codes
- Form 5498 Box 1 for IRA contributions
- AGI and filing status
- Any retirement distributions received in the lookback period
- Age and student status
## Questions
- Did you contribute to an IRA or a workplace retirement plan?
- What is your AGI and filing status?
- Did you take any retirement distributions in the last three years?
- Are you a full-time student or claimed as someone's dependent?
## Common Errors
- Overlooking the credit entirely — it is one of the least-claimed credits relative to eligibility
- Forgetting to reduce contributions by recent distributions
- Claiming it as a full-time student or a dependent
- Expecting a refund from a nonrefundable credit
## Prompt
- I contributed to my 401(k) and my income is modest.
- I put $2,000 in a Roth IRA this year.
- Is there a credit for saving for retirement?
