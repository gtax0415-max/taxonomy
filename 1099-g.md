# Form 1099-G
## Description
Certain government payments. For income purposes, the most common is unemployment compensation (Box 1). Form 1099-G is also used to report state or local income tax refunds (Box 2).
## Notes
- Unemployment compensation (Box 1) is fully taxable at the federal level and goes on Schedule 1, Line 7, then to Form 1040, Line 8
- A state or local tax refund (Box 2) is only taxable if you itemized deductions in the prior year and deducted state income tax
- Federal tax may have been withheld from unemployment (Box 4) — claim it as a payment
## Required Information
- Payer (state agency) name and TIN
- Unemployment compensation (Box 1)
- State/local tax refund (Box 2), if applicable
- Federal income tax withheld (Box 4)
## Questions
- Did you receive unemployment compensation this year?
- Did you have federal tax withheld from it (Box 4)?
- Did you receive a state tax refund, and did you itemize last year?
## Common Errors
- Not reporting unemployment because no tax was withheld
- Treating a state tax refund as taxable when the standard deduction was taken last year
- Forgetting to claim Box 4 withholding as a payment
## Prompt
- I collected unemployment benefits and received a 1099-G.
- I was laid off and got unemployment compensation on a 1099-G.
- My 1099-G shows a state tax refund.
