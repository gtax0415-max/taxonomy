# Cash and Unreported Business Income
## Description
Self-employment income received in cash or otherwise not reported on a 1099-NEC, 1099-K, or 1099-MISC. This income is still fully taxable and must be reported.
## Notes
- Report all gross receipts on Schedule C, Line 1 — including cash tips, cash gigs, and small jobs where no form was issued
- Just because no 1099 was received does not make the income tax-free
## Required Information
- Description of the activity
- Total gross receipts (including amounts with no 1099)
- Related business expenses
## Questions
- Did you earn any cash income or income for which you did not receive a 1099?
- What was the total amount, and do you have records?
- Do you have business expenses to offset this income?
## Common Errors
- Omitting cash income because no form was issued
- Failing to keep records of cash receipts
- Forgetting self-employment tax on the net profit
## Prompt
- I earned some money from cash gigs this year with no 1099.
- I got paid in cash for side jobs.
- I have self-employment income that was not reported on any form.
