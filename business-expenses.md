# Business Expenses (Schedule C)
## Description
Ordinary and necessary expenses of operating a sole proprietorship, deducted on Schedule C Part II. These reduce business income BEFORE it reaches Form 1040, so they are not part of the standard/itemized choice at all.
## Where these sit in the return
Unlike itemized deductions, business expenses reduce income at the source: Schedule C gross receipts minus expenses = net profit (Schedule C Line 31), which flows to Schedule 1 Line 3 and then Form 1040 Line 8. You get them whether you take the standard deduction or itemize.
## Scale (TY 2022)
About 35.3 million returns file Schedule C — the most-filed schedule after Schedule 1. The most common expense lines are car and truck expenses (13.7M), office expense (11.2M), utilities (6.8M), legal and professional services (6.5M), depreciation (4.7M), and business use of home (3.5M).

## Notes
- An expense must be both ORDINARY (common in your trade) and NECESSARY (helpful and appropriate) to be deductible
- Personal expenses are never deductible; mixed-use items must be split by business percentage
- Reducing net profit also reduces self-employment tax, unlike most other deductions
- Good contemporaneous records are essential — most of these expenses have no third-party form
