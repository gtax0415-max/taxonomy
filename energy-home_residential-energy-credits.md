# Residential Energy Credits (Form 5695)
## Description
Two nonrefundable credits for improving a home's energy performance, both TERMINATED by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act after December 31, 2025. Form 5695 appeared on about 3.4 million returns for TY 2022 (IRS SOI); that volume drops to essentially zero for 2026.
## Status for 2026 — both credits are gone
- Section 25C, Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit: terminated for property placed in service AFTER December 31, 2025
- Section 25D, Residential Clean Energy Credit: terminated for expenditures made AFTER December 31, 2025
Originally 25C ran through 2032 and 25D through 2034. OBBBA accelerated both by seven to nine years.
## What they covered while available
SECTION 25C — 30% of the cost, with annual caps by item:
- Heat pumps and heat pump water heaters: $2,000
- Insulation and air sealing: $1,200
- Exterior windows: $600
- Exterior doors: $500
- Electrical panel upgrades: $600
- Home energy audits: $150
- Overall annual cap: $3,200
- Applied to an existing main home; annual (not lifetime) caps, so work could be spread across years
- Required a Qualified Manufacturer Identification Number for the equipment
SECTION 25D — 30% of the cost with NO dollar cap:
- Solar electric panels, solar water heating
- Small wind energy property
- Geothermal heat pumps
- Fuel cell property (subject to a per-kilowatt limit)
- Battery storage technology
- Available for a main home and, for most property types, a second home. New construction qualified
- Unused credit could be CARRIED FORWARD to future years
## The timing trap
For both credits, the test is when the property was PLACED IN SERVICE, not when it was ordered, paid for, or delivered. A system paid for in December 2025 but energized in January 2026 gets nothing.
## Carryforward
Unused Section 25D credit from 2025 or earlier can still be carried forward and used on later returns, even though no new credit can be generated. Section 25C had no carryforward.
## Required Information
- Invoices showing the placed-in-service date
- Manufacturer certification statements
- QMID for Section 25C equipment
- Cost allocated between qualifying and non-qualifying work
- Any Section 25D carryforward from a prior year
## Questions
- When was the property PLACED IN SERVICE (on or before December 31, 2025)?
- Which type of property was it — 25C efficiency upgrades or 25D clean energy?
- Was it your main home, and is it existing or new construction?
- Do you have a Section 25D carryforward from an earlier year?
## Common Errors
- Assuming a 2026 installation still qualifies
- Using the payment date instead of the placed-in-service date
- Missing the per-item annual caps under Section 25C
- Overlooking a Section 25D carryforward from an earlier year
- Confusing federal credits with state or utility rebates, which continue
## Prompt
- I installed solar panels on my house.
- I replaced my furnace with a heat pump.
- I added insulation and new windows.
