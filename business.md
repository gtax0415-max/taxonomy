# Business Income
## Description
Self-employment and business income for sole proprietors, independent contractors, gig workers, and pass-through owners. Most sole-proprietor income is totaled on Schedule C and flows through Schedule 1, Line 3 to Form 1040, Line 8. Individual source documents are detailed in the files below.
## Forms
- Form 1099-NEC — Nonemployee compensation (1099-nec.md)
- Form 1099-K — Payment card and third-party network payments (1099-k.md)
- Form 1099-MISC — Rents, royalties, prizes, other income (1099-misc.md)
- Cash and unreported income — Income with no 1099 issued (cash-income.md)
- Schedule K-1 — Partnership and S-corporation income (schedule-k1.md)
## Notes
- All business income is taxable whether or not a 1099 was issued
- Net profit (after expenses on Schedule C) is generally subject to self-employment tax
- Qualifying pass-through income may be eligible for the QBI deduction
## Questions
- What kind of business or self-employment income did you have?
- Which forms did you receive (1099-NEC, 1099-K, 1099-MISC, K-1), if any?
- Do you have records of business expenses to deduct?
## Prompt
- I have income from self-employment or a side business.
- I received several 1099 forms for freelance and gig work.
- I run a small business and need to report my income.
