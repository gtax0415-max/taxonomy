# Qualified Passenger Vehicle Loan Interest (2025-2028)
## Description
Deduction of up to $10,000 per year for interest paid on a loan used to buy a new, U.S.-assembled passenger vehicle for personal use. Created by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act for tax years 2025 through 2028. Commonly called "no tax on car loan interest."
## Notes
- Maximum deduction: $10,000 PER RETURN — this cap is NOT doubled for married couples filing jointly. Single and joint filers have the same $10,000 limit
- Phases out when MAGI exceeds $100,000 ($200,000 married filing jointly). The deduction is reduced by $200 for each $1,000 of MAGI over the threshold, so it is fully gone at $150,000 single / $250,000 joint
- Available whether you take the standard deduction or itemize
- Temporary: tax years 2025 through 2028 only
- Applies retroactively to interest paid after December 31, 2024
## Vehicle requirements (all must be met)
- NEW vehicle — original use must begin with you. Used vehicles never qualify, no matter how new to you
- FINAL ASSEMBLY IN THE UNITED STATES. Verify using the vehicle information label at the dealership, the VIN, or the NHTSA VIN Decoder tool
- Car, minivan, van, SUV, pickup truck, or motorcycle
- Gross vehicle weight rating under 14,000 pounds
- Manufactured primarily for use on public streets, roads, and highways
- Used for PERSONAL purposes. Vehicles used for business, commercial, or fleet purposes generally do not qualify (other than use as an employee)
## Loan requirements
- The loan must ORIGINATE AFTER DECEMBER 31, 2024
- Must be a FIRST LIEN SECURED BY the vehicle purchased
- LEASES DO NOT QUALIFY — lease payments are not deductible interest
- If a qualifying loan is later refinanced, interest on the refinanced loan is generally still eligible
## Reporting requirement
You must report the vehicle's VIN on your tax return for EACH year you claim the deduction. Missing the VIN can cost you the deduction.
## Required Information
- Interest paid during the year (lender statement)
- Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)
- Confirmation of U.S. final assembly
- Loan origination date
- MAGI and filing status (for the phase-out)
## Questions
- Did you buy a NEW vehicle with a loan taken out after December 31, 2024?
- Was the vehicle's final assembly in the United States (check the VIN or NHTSA decoder)?
- Is the vehicle for personal use rather than business or fleet use?
- Is it a loan secured by the vehicle, or a lease (leases do not qualify)?
- Is your MAGI above $100,000 ($200,000 joint)?
- Do you have the VIN to report on the return?
## Common Errors
- Claiming the deduction on a USED vehicle
- Claiming it on a LEASED vehicle
- Assuming any American-brand vehicle qualifies — what matters is the final assembly location, not the brand
- Claiming it on a loan taken out before 2025
- Omitting the VIN from the return
- Assuming the $10,000 cap doubles for joint filers (it does not)
- Claiming it on an unsecured personal loan or a home equity loan used to buy the car
## Prompt
- I bought a new car this year and have a car loan.
- Can I deduct the interest on my auto loan?
- I financed a new truck made in America.
