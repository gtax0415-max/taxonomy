# Credits
## Description
Credits reduce TAX directly, dollar for dollar. A deduction reduces the income the tax is calculated on; a $1,000 credit cuts the tax itself by $1,000, which is why credits are usually worth more than deductions of the same size.
## Refundable vs nonrefundable — the key distinction
- NONREFUNDABLE credits can reduce your tax to zero but no further. Anything left over is lost (some carry forward). They flow through Schedule 3, Part I to Form 1040 Line 20
- REFUNDABLE credits can go BELOW zero and produce a refund even if you owed no tax. They flow through Schedule 3, Part II to Form 1040 Line 31, or onto their own Form 1040 line
- Some credits are PARTIALLY refundable: the Child Tax Credit (refundable portion is the Additional Child Tax Credit), the American Opportunity Credit (40% refundable), and, beginning in 2025, the Adoption Credit (up to $5,000 refundable)
## Where credits land on Form 1040
- Line 19 — Child tax credit and credit for other dependents (Schedule 8812)
- Line 20 — Total nonrefundable credits from Schedule 3, Part I
- Line 27 — Earned income credit
- Line 28 — Additional child tax credit (refundable part, Schedule 8812)
- Line 29 — American opportunity credit refundable portion (Form 8863)
- Line 31 — Total refundable credits and other payments from Schedule 3, Part II

- Schedule EIC, earned income credit: ~17.2M returns
- Form 8880, saver's credit: ~9.4M returns

- Form 2441, child and dependent care: ~6.5M returns
